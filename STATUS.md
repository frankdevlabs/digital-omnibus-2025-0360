## One-line status

Council moving from working party to **a negotiating mandate**: the Cyprus Presidency has circulated its
**fifth compromise, ST 10729/26 (22 June 2026)**, *as the mandate for trilogue negotiations*, for
**confirmation at Coreper-II on 26 June 2026** (before the 1 July Irish handover). Parliament has now
moved beyond committee referral: the **lead committees' joint ITRE/LIBE draft report** (PE786.818,
22 Jun) is **tabled** — its first cross-strand position, walking back the deregulation (restores P2B
and GDPR Art 22, EDPB-authored templates/lists); **no trilogue yet**.
The mandate adds — late, at delegations' request and with no impact assessment — a **GDPR Article 49 +
recital 40b derogation for FATCA-style tax-data transfers to third countries** ([analysis](docs/provisions/gdpr-art49-third-country-tax-transfers.md)).
Coreper had not yet confirmed the mandate as of this update (a text circulated to Coreper, not yet a Council act).
**Update (26 Jun):** the Digital Omnibus mandate item (ST 10729/26) was **withdrawn from the Friday 26 June Council
(General Affairs) agenda** — the sign-off **did not proceed as scheduled** and Council confirmation slips (likely toward
the Irish Presidency, H2 2026). Surfaced via Bluesky (`T3-01`) with an embedded agenda screenshot; **not yet** confirmed
by the live Council register / Coreper outcome (`T1-08`/`T1-09`, WAF-gated) — [triage #90](docs/triage/2026-06-26-issue-90.md).

## Where each institution stands

- **Stage:** Proposal adopted by the College 19 Nov 2025; now defends the text.
- **Latest act:** COM(2025) 837 final + SWD(2025) 836 — [digest](docs/commission-proposal.md) · [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/procedure/EN/2025_360)

### Council of the EU — co-legislator
- **Stage:** Moving from working party (**Antici Group on Simplification**, with DATAPROTECT / CYBER / TELECOM / COMPET input) to **Coreper**: the Presidency has put its compromise to the Permanent Representatives Committee as a **mandate for trilogue negotiations**.
- **Latest act:** **ST 10729/26** (22 June 2026) — *mandate for negotiations with the European Parliament*, the fifth and latest Cyprus compromise, after [ST 9547/26](sources/README.md) (21 May), the interim ST 10677/26 (18 Jun, not held) and earlier revs. [Operative extracts](extracts/council/) · [what changed](docs/what-changed.md). Per the Presidency NOTE the text amends **fourteen GDPR articles**.
- **Owner:** Cyprus Presidency (Jan–Jun 2026) → Irish Presidency (from 1 Jul 2026).
- **Position:** Deletes the Art 4 redefinition and the standalone AI legitimate-interest article; keeps cookies in ePrivacy; **newly adds a GDPR Art 49 + recital 40b derogation for periodic/automated tax-data transfers to third countries** (FATCA-style) — see *What changed* below + [`docs/provisions/gdpr-art49-third-country-tax-transfers.md`](docs/provisions/gdpr-art49-third-country-tax-transfers.md) + [`data/positions.csv`](data/positions.csv).

Confirmation thus **slips** — the mandate / a general approach would fall to the **Irish Presidency (H2 2026)** after the 1 July handover. ### European Parliament — co-legislator
- **Stage:** Lead committees' **joint draft report tabled** (22 Jun 2026); two committee-for-opinion texts also drafted — [Oeil](https://oeil.europarl.europa.eu/oeil/en/procedure-file?reference=2025/0360(COD)) status ***Awaiting committee decision***. **No committee vote yet.**

Earlier, two committee-for-opinion draft opinions: **IMCO** (Agius Saliba, S&D) — **PE789.877v01-00**, **124 amendments** ([extract](extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md) · [triage #61](docs/triage/2026-06-18-issue-61.md)); and **JURI** (Benifei, S&D) — **PE789.142v01-00**, 68 amendments ([extract](extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md) · [triage #47](docs/triage/2026-06-15-issue-47.md)), both opposing the **P2B repeal** and keeping **smart contracts** (Art 36 Data Act); IMCO additionally **restores the current cloud-switching regime** and tightens trade-secret/B2G/enforcement safeguards (see [Data Act strand](docs/instruments/data-act-2023-2854.md#parliament-imco-draft-opinion)).
