# EESC opinion — INT/1108 (Digital Omnibus)

A digest of the **European Economic and Social Committee** opinion on the *Digital Omnibus* package. The
opinion is a **single instrument covering both** Omnibus proposals — the AI strand
(**COM(2025) 836 / 2025/0359 (COD)**) and the data/cyber strand this repo tracks
(**COM(2025) 837 / 2025/0360 (COD)**). Only the 2025/0360 points are this repo's concern; the AI points are
summarised briefly for context.

## Document facts

- **Reference:** INT/1108 (EESC document EESC-2025-03929-AS); rapporteur **Heiko Willems**, co-rapporteur
  **Angelo Pagliara**.
- **Section:** Single Market, Production and Consumption (INT). **Adopted in section 11 February 2026**
  (102/0/1). *(The repo timeline records the plenary adoption date 18 March 2026 — confirm the final plenary
  vote against the authoritative version.)*
- **Legal basis cited:** Article 114 TFEU. Referred by the European Parliament (5 Dec 2025) and the Council
  (3 Feb 2026).

## Overall stance

The EESC **takes note of and broadly supports** the simplification objective as *"a first step in the right
direction"*, provided it does **not lower standards**: it must *"maintain essential and relevant frameworks of
the GDPR and the AI Act"* and a high level of fundamental-rights protection (Art 8(1) Charter, Art 16(1)
TFEU). Simplification should be developed **in partnership with** companies, workers, consumers, the
scientific community, NGOs and civil society, respecting better-regulation principles. It pairs *"simplification
by design"* with a *"rights by design"* approach.

## Key points on the data/cyber Omnibus (2025/0360)

- **Single entry point (SEP):** strong support for a *genuinely interoperable* SEP covering NIS2, GDPR, DORA,
  eIDAS and CER; it should allow **English-language** submissions, **reuse of previously submitted
  information**, and strict **need-to-know** access controls; pilots co-designed with industry and the social
  partners. → [cyber extract](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_cyber-art6-9.md)
- **Definition of personal data (Art 4(1)):** *decisive* for the scope of the GDPR; must be **objective and
  adequately broad**, ensure legal certainty and prevent *"subjective or evasive interpretations"*. The
  explanatory notes invoke CJEU case-law that the notion must be interpreted **broadly**. →
  [Art 4 provision](../provisions/gdpr-art4-personal-data.md),
  [GDPR extract point 1](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-1--article-4-definitions)
- **Pseudonymisation / anonymisation:** supports Commission **implementing acts** clarifying when
  pseudonymised data may be treated as anonymised for other entities — important for AI training while keeping
  privacy safeguards.
- **Right of access:** an *enabling right*; should be **fully ensured** where the data subject pursues a
  legitimate purpose (esp. workers' rights, health and safety); the **burden to prove abuse stays with the
  controller**. → [Art 12/57 provision](../provisions/gdpr-art12-57-abusive-requests.md)
- **Scientific research:** the definition should keep its **commonly accepted meaning** (per EDPB and CJEU
  case-law) while enabling industrial research; warns against broad/absolute limitations of Charter Arts 7–8.
- **Article 22 (automated decisions):** must respect **data minimisation** (Art 5(1)); ADM only where it adds
  clear value and is necessary and proportionate; **human decision-making preferred** where reasonable. →
  [Art 22 provision](../provisions/gdpr-art22-automated-decisions.md)
- **ePrivacy / consent:** encourages exploring **simplified consent in low-risk contexts** via a proportionate,
  risk-based solution, preserving privacy protection. → [cookies provision](../provisions/gdpr-art88a-88b-cookies.md)
- **SMEs / SMCs:** supports extending simplification (templates, examples, streamlined processes — mandatory
  where no personal data are involved) to **small mid-caps**, but **only for autonomous undertakings** (not
  partner/linked enterprises, per Recommendations 2003/361/EC and 2025/1099).
- **Data Act:** mandatory data-sharing should be limited to where **strictly necessary** (e.g. public
  emergencies, workers'/fundamental-rights protection); the Commission should focus on **incentives for
  voluntary data sharing**, strengthen the **European Data Innovation Board**, and foster common European data
  spaces. → [Data Act extract](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_dataact-art1.md)
- **Definitions generally:** calls for clear, harmonised definitions of *'personal data'*, *'data holder'*,
  *'user'*, *'data processing service'* and *'placing on the market'* across all relevant acts; where conflicts
  arise, **safety/security legislation should take precedence** over data-sharing obligations.

## On the AI Omnibus (2025/0359) — for context only

Supports risk-based, proportionate regulation (lighter for industrial/B2B uses with safeguards); a possible
**intra-group "corporate privilege"**; mandatory **involvement of the social partners** before introducing AI
in the workplace; aligning high-risk application deadlines with **harmonised standards** (CEN/CENELEC) and
notified-body capacity; **regulatory sandboxes**; a well-resourced **AI Office**; AI literacy obligations
(Art 4 AI Act); and **timely conclusion of the AI-Omnibus trilogue** before the August 2026 high-risk
deadlines.
