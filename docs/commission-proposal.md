# The Commission proposal — COM(2025) 837 final (explanatory memorandum)

A plain-language digest of the **explanatory memorandum** of the Commission's *Digital Omnibus* proposal
(**COM(2025) 837 final**, 19 November 2025, file 2025/0360 (COD)). The operative text is transcribed in
[`../extracts/commission/`](../extracts/commission/); this page captures the proposal's *own narrative*
(objectives, legal basis, the instruments touched). For where the file stands now and what the Council
changed, see [`../STATUS.md`](../STATUS.md) and [`institutional-positions.md`](institutional-positions.md).

## Why the Commission tabled it

The proposal is framed as the **first, immediate step** of the Commission's *"simpler and faster Europe"*
simplification agenda (Communication COM(2025) 47), responding to the **Draghi** and **Letta** competitiveness
reports and successive European Council conclusions (20 March, 26 June, 23 October 2025) calling for
**"simplicity by design"** legislation *"without undermining predictability, policy goals, and high
standards"*. The College adopted it on **19 November 2025** alongside SWD(2025) 836; it is one of the two
*Digital Omnibus* proposals (the other, **COM(2025) 836 / 2025/0359 (COD)**, amends the **AI Act** and runs on
a separate, faster track). Owners: EVP **Henna Virkkunen** (Tech Sovereignty) and Commissioner **Michael
McGrath** (Justice), with DG CNECT.

The memorandum stresses the changes are **technical**, leaving the underlying objectives and the level of
fundamental-rights protection intact, while the broader **Digital Fitness Check** (launched in parallel) looks
at cumulative impact for a possible second-half-of-mandate package.

## Legal basis, subsidiarity, proportionality

- **Legal basis:** Articles **16** and **114 TFEU** — Article 16 for the GDPR (Art 3) and EUDPR (Art 4)
  amendments; Article 114 for all the other amended acts, reflecting their own legal bases.
- **Subsidiarity:** the amended rules are Union rules and can only be amended at Union level; the technical
  adjustments preserve the existing subsidiarity logic and leave Member-State and EU-body competences unchanged.
- **Proportionality:** the amendments are presented as targeted and calibrated; the headline ambition is an
  administrative-burden reduction of **≥25% (35% for SMEs)**, including extending SME facilitations to **small
  mid-caps (SMCs)**.

## What the proposal does, by instrument

The enacting terms run to **11 articles** (full map in
[`../extracts/commission/`](../extracts/commission/)):

| Art | Instrument | Thrust | Extract |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Data Act** (EU) 2023/2854 | Consolidates the **DGA**, **FFNPD** and **Open Data Directive** into the Data Act; calibrates trade-secret refusal, narrows B2G to "public emergencies", eases switching for custom/SME services, deletes smart-contract rules | [Data Act](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_dataact-art1.md) |
| 2 | **Single Digital Gateway** (EU) 2018/1724 | Adds data-intermediation / data-altruism registration procedures to the Annex II table | [SDG](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_sdg-art2.md) |
| 3 | **GDPR** (EU) 2016/679 | Relative personal-data clause; scientific-research definition; AI & biometric Art 9 carve-outs; abusive-request fees; transparency exemptions; **Art 22 rewrite**; 96h/high-risk breach notification; EU-level DPIA list; pseudonymisation implementing power; **new Arts 88a (cookies→GDPR), 88b (machine-readable consent), 88c (AI legitimate interest)** | [GDPR](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md) |
| 4 | **EUDPR** (EU) 2018/1725 | Mirrors the GDPR changes for EU institutions | [EUDPR](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_eudpr-art4.md) |
| 5 | **ePrivacy Directive** 2002/58/EC | Deletes Art 4; carves natural-person personal-data processing out of Art 5(3) (it moves to GDPR Art 88a) | [ePrivacy](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_eprivacy-art5.md) |
| 6–9 | **NIS2 / eIDAS / DORA / CER** | **Single-entry point** for incident reporting (ENISA-built), "report once, share many" | [Cyber](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_cyber-art6-9.md) |
| 10–11 | Repeals & final | **Repeals** P2B (2019/1150), DGA (2022/868), FFNPD (2018/1807), Open Data Dir (2019/1024); staggered application | [Final](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_final-art10-11.md) |

The proposal also carries **60 recitals** (curated subset in
[recitals](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_recitals.md)) and an **Annex I correlation table** (committed
separately).

## Consistency with other measures

The memorandum situates the proposal alongside the **AI-Act Omnibus** (COM(2025) 836), the forthcoming
**Cybersecurity Act revision** (Reg (EU) 2019/881, incl. ENISA's mandate), and the **European Business
Wallets** Regulation (with which the single-entry point is to be interoperable).
