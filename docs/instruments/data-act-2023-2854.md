# Instrument: Data Act — Regulation (EU) 2023/2854

Amended by **Article 1** of the Omnibus — but **only in the April text, ST 8261/1/26 REV 1**. February
(ST 6406/26) and May (ST 9547/26) contain no Omnibus Article 1; the Data Act is merely cover-listed there.
Operative text (April):
[../../extracts/council/ST-8261-1-26-REV1_dataact-art1.md](../../extracts/council/ST-8261-1-26-REV1_dataact-art1.md).

## What Article 1 does

The Omnibus turns the Data Act into the **consolidating home** for several data-acquis instruments that
Article 10 repeals (the **Data Governance Act (EU) 2022/868**, the **Free Flow of Non-Personal Data
Regulation (EU) 2018/1807** and the **Open Data Directive (EU) 2019/1024** — see
[repealed instruments](repealed.md)). It amends a set of existing Data Act articles (Article 1 scope,
Article 2 definitions, Articles 17–21, Article 32) and **inserts three new chapters**:

| Inserted block | Articles | Subject (high level) |
|---|---|---|
| **Chapter VIIa** | 32a–32g | Data intermediation services (relocated from the DGA) |
| **Chapter VIIb** | 32h | Data altruism (relocated from the DGA) |
| **Chapter VIIc** | 32i–32z | Re-use of certain categories of protected data held by public sector bodies / open data (relocated from the DGA and the Open Data Directive), the European Data Innovation Board, and related procedures |

## Parliament — JURI draft opinion (PE789.142, Benifei, 5 Jun 2026)

The **first formal EP committee text** on the file, and the first detailed Parliament position on the
data-acquis strand: a JURI draft opinion **for the lead joint ITRE/LIBE** with **68 amendments**, almost
all targeting this instrument. Its through-line is to **reverse much of the Omnibus's deregulation** of the
Data Act / DGA / Open Data acquis. It amends the **Commission proposal** COM(2025) 837 (note the Council's
May text, ST 9547/26, carries no Article 1, so JURI and the latest Council text are not directly comparable
here). Full operative text:
[JURI draft opinion extract](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md). Key threads:

- **Trade secrets** — deletes the new third-country trade-secret refusal ground and its recitals
  (Am 1–5), and rewrites Data Act Art 4(8)/5(11) so refusal stays a **last resort** behind proportionate
  confidentiality measures plus EDIB guidance
  ([Am 26](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-26),
  [Am 27](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-27)).
- **B2G / Chapter V** — opposes deleting Data Act **Arts 14 and 15**
  ([Am 28](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-28)), removes "mitigation"
  from the emergency trigger and pseudonymises emergency data (Am 29–30), and **inserts a new Art 15b**
  for public-interest access **outside** emergencies with safeguards
  ([Am 31](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-31)).
- **Data intermediation / altruism (ex-DGA)** — restores the original DGA **data-intermediation-service**
  definition ([Am 21](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-21)), reinstates
  **mandatory** registration ("shall", vs the Omnibus's voluntary registration — Am 13–14,
  [Am 47](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-47),
  [Am 48](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-48)), and adds **neutrality**
  duties: a ban on secondary use (ads/profiling/AI training) of intermediated data, fairness, logging and
  security ([Am 43–46](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-43)).
- **Definitions** — deletes the new "access", "bodies governed by public law" and "public undertaking"
  definitions (Am 15–18); reworks the "public emergency" definition to distinguish response from
  mitigation/recovery ([Am 19](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-19)).
- **Smart contracts** — opposes deleting Data Act **Art 36**, keeping minimum security/interoperability
  safeguards ([Am 36](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-36)).
- **Cloud switching** — opposes the new SME/custom-service switching exemptions (Art 31(1a)/(1b))
  ([Am 34](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-34)).
- **Open data** — preserves open-data principles, no new disclosure obligation, default open licences
  (Am 56–60); **EDIB** role strengthened on cross-border cooperation, guidance and standardisation
  (Am 62–66).
- **P2B repeal** — opposes it outright; see [Repeals & final provisions](../provisions/final-repeals-p2b.md#parliament--juri-draft-opinion).

Surfaced via triage [#47](../triage/2026-06-15-issue-47.md). This is a committee-**for-opinion** text, not
the joint ITRE/LIBE draft report (still pending); amendment-tabling deadline 26 Jun 2026.

## Advisory positions

- **ECB (CON/2026/9)** — welcomes simplification of G2B/B2G data sharing (NCBs and the ECB benefit from
  clearer access to **high-value datasets** used for statistics); supports **narrowing** the new
  "exceptional need" power (Chapter VIIc / Art 15a) but asks to **simplify the conditions** public
  authorities must meet and to **clarify "public emergency"** (align with Reg (EU) 2024/2747 IMERA); on
  compensation, public-interest data should be supplied **at cost**, without a "reasonable margin".
  → [digest](../advisory/ecb-con-2026-9.md)
- **EESC (INT/1108)** — mandatory data-sharing should be limited to where **strictly necessary** (public
  emergencies, workers'/fundamental-rights protection); the Commission should prioritise **incentives for
  voluntary sharing**, **strengthen the European Data Innovation Board**, and foster common European data
  spaces. → [digest](../advisory/eesc-int1108.md)
