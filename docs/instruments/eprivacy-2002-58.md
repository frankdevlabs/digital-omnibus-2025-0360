# Instrument: ePrivacy Directive — Directive 2002/58/EC

Amended by **Article 5** of the Omnibus. Operative text:
[../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md](../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md).

| Omnibus point | ePrivacy article | Change | ST 9547/26 outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Art 4 | Security & breach provisions | **Deleted** (now covered by GDPR Arts 32–34 / NIS2) |
| 2 | Art 5(3) | Terminal-equipment consent | **Replaced** — consent rule + exempt purposes + single-click refusal + **6-month no-repeat**; GDPR DPA designated as enforcer |
| 2a | Art 17 | Transposition deadline | New para — transpose Art 5(3) within 24 months |

**Key point:** because the Council **deleted GDPR Art 88a**, the cookie/terminal-equipment consent regime
**stays in this Directive** (amended Art 5(3)). This is the home of the consent-exempt purposes
(transmission; explicitly requested service; anonymous first-party audience measurement; security incl.
fraud prevention) and the anti-consent-fatigue rule. See
[Cookies provision](../provisions/gdpr-art88a-88b-cookies.md).

The machine-readable consent **signals** article, by contrast, sits in the **GDPR** (new Art 88a, formerly
88b).

## Parliament — IMCO draft opinion (PE789.877, Agius Saliba, 8 Jun 2026)
The Omnibus (its **Article 5, point 1**) deletes **ePrivacy Art 4** (security of processing). The **IMCO
draft opinion** marks that amendment as **"deleted"**
([Am 122](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-122)) — i.e. it **opposes the
deletion of ePrivacy Art 4 and keeps it in force**. A committee-for-opinion text, not the joint ITRE/LIBE
draft report (still pending); amendment deadline 8 Jul 2026. Via triage
[#61](../triage/2026-06-18-issue-61.md). See the
[Cookies provision](../provisions/gdpr-art88a-88b-cookies.md#parliament-imco-draft-opinion).
