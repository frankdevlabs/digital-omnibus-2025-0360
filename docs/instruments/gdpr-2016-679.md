## Parallel amendment by another file — Omnibus IV (2025/0130, agreed 26 Jun 2026)

The GDPR is **simultaneously being amended by a second, faster legislative file**: **Omnibus IV**
(2025/0130 (COD), the SME→small-mid-cap simplification regulation). Coreper confirmed the agreed
compromise texts and the offer letter to the Parliament on **26 June 2026** (information note
[ST 10978/26](../../sources/README.md#council-documents) — registered as a *related-file* document).
Its Article 1 amends, on this file's territory:

| GDPR article | Omnibus IV change | Interplay with 2025/0360 |
|---|---|---|
| Art 4 — new points (27), (28) | Adds **SME** (Rec 2003/361) and **small mid-cap (SMC)** (<1000 employees, ≤€200m turnover or ≤€172m balance sheet, Rec 2025/1099) definitions | Same article this file's point 1 amends ([Art 4 provision page](../provisions/gdpr-art4-personal-data.md)) — numbering/consolidation dependency for the trilogue text |
| Art 30(5) | Records-of-processing derogation extended **<250 → <1000 employees**, unless a specific processing activity is likely high-risk (Art 35) and always where it is a DPO-core-activity (Art 37(1)(b)–(c)); obligations then apply only to that activity | Art 30 is **not touched** by 2025/0360, but the derogation reshapes the SME/SMC compliance baseline the omnibus's own simplifications are argued from |
| Arts 40(1), 42(1) | Codes of conduct and certification to take **SMC** needs into account | Context for this file's EDPB governance/codes strand |

Whichever file completes first, the later text must consolidate onto the other's amended base —
watch for numbering conflicts on Art 4 when 2025/0360 reaches trilogue.
