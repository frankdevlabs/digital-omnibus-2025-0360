# EUDPR (Reg (EU) 2018/1725) — the EU-institutions mirror — Omnibus Art 4

**Tracked because:** the EUDPR governs data protection by the **EU institutions, bodies, offices and
agencies**, and is meant to track the GDPR changes one-for-one so the two regimes stay homogeneous. The
Commission proposed a full mirror; the **Council dropped the operative EUDPR article**, leaving a coherence
gap that the ECB and the regulators have flagged.

## Current law
Regulation (EU) 2018/1725 mirrors the GDPR for the EU institutions; the **European Data Protection Supervisor
(EDPS)** is the competent supervisory authority. Its provisions parallel the GDPR articles amended elsewhere
in this package.

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837, Article 4)
A point-for-point mirror of the [GDPR (Article 3) amendments](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md):
- **Art 3 (definitions)** — the same **relative personal-data** sentences as GDPR Art 4(1); electronic-
  communications-networks; new **'mobile application'**, **'online interface'**, **'scientific research'**.
- **Art 4(1)(b)** — purpose limitation (research/archiving/statistics compatible, independent of Art 6).
- **Art 10** — special categories: new points **(k)** (AI development/operation) and **(l)** (biometric
  verification) and a new **paragraph 4** mirroring GDPR Art 9(5).
- **Art 14(5)** — abusive requests: free-of-charge information; **refuse to act** on abusive Art 17 requests
  (no "reasonable fee" option here); burden on the controller.
- **Art 15(5)** — research exemption from the duty to inform.
- **Art 24** — automated decisions, including the **"regardless of whether the decision could be taken
  otherwise than by solely automated means"** clause.
- **Art 34(1)** — breach notification: high-risk-only; **96 hours**; notify the **EDPS** (here, **not** via
  the single-entry point).
- **Art 37** — terminal-equipment processing & machine-readable signals (mirrors GDPR Arts 88a/88b).
- **Art 39** — DPIA alignment (the GDPR Art 35(6a) lists apply; Art 39(5)/(6) deleted).
- **New Art 45a** — pseudonymisation alignment (the GDPR Art 41a criteria apply).
- → [Commission text — EUDPR Art 4](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_eudpr-art4.md)

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Not operatively amended.** ST 9547/26 references the EUDPR only **in the recitals** as a parallel-
  amendment *intention*; the operative EUDPR article was dropped, with alignment deferred to a **later
  alignment exercise** (recital 43). There is therefore **no Council EUDPR extract** under
  [`extracts/council/`](../../extracts/council/). The EUDPR mirror would follow the GDPR text once that is
  settled (and would inherit the Council's GDPR retreats — deleted Art 4(1) clause, deleted AI Art 88c,
  cookie regime kept in ePrivacy, Art 22 restored, EDPB-led lists/templates).
- → [Council GDPR text (the parent track)](../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md)

## Parliament (provisional)
No committee amendment touches the EUDPR mirror (Omnibus Art 4): neither the JURI (PE789.142) nor the IMCO
(PE789.877) draft opinion addresses it, and the lead joint ITRE/LIBE draft report is pending (Oeil: "Awaiting
committee decision"). The provision is therefore open. Note the EDPB-EDPS position below that, unless otherwise
stated, their GDPR comments apply equally to the corresponding EUDPR amendments (¶10) — so the Parliament
outcome on the EUDPR mirror should track whatever the joint report does on the parent GDPR track.

## EDPB–EDPS
The Joint Opinion 2/2026 states that, unless noted, its **GDPR comments apply equally to the corresponding
EUDPR amendments** (¶10). Specific EUDPR points: **mirror the breach changes in EUDPR Art 34(1)** (¶87);
**align EUDPR Art 15(4)** with the Art 13(4) transparency result (¶64); the **EUDPR Art 39 DPIA amendment is
neither necessary nor appropriate** (the EDPS already adopts EUI DPIA lists) and **should not be adopted**,
with any EUDPR methodology adopted by the **EDPS** (¶91, ¶95); and entrust the **EDPS** with approving the
EUDPR-equivalent templates/lists.
→ [JO 2/2026: DPIA](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#dpia),
[breach notification](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#breach-notification)

## ECB
**ECB (CON/2026/9)** — the ECB processes under the **EUDPR** while NCBs/NCAs process under the **GDPR**, so a
single operation is governed by both; it **welcomes the GDPR and EUDPR being amended in parallel** for
coherence, and recommends **including the EUDPR among the acts for which the single-entry point must be used**
(today the ECB still reports breaches to the EDPS via the EUDPR channel). → [digest](../advisory/ecb-con-2026-9.md)

## Status
**In limbo.** The Commission proposed a full EUDPR mirror; the Council dropped the operative article and
deferred alignment, opening a temporary GDPR/EUDPR coherence gap that the ECB (parallel amendment; EUDPR in
the SEP) and the EDPB–EDPS (Art 39; Art 34(1); Art 15(4)) want closed.

## Member-State signals
None specific — the EUDPR concerns the EU institutions rather than Member-State competence, so the WK 3736/26
delegation comments do not address it directly.

## Open questions
- When and how the deferred **EUDPR alignment exercise** happens, and which Council GDPR outcomes it inherits.
- Whether the **EUDPR is added to the single-entry point** (ECB ask).
- Whether the **EDPS** gains approval power over EUI DPIA lists/templates (EDPB–EDPS ask).
