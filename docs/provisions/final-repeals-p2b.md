# Repeals, transitional & final provisions — Omnibus Arts 10–11 (incl. P2B)

**Tracked because:** Article 10 decides whether the **Platform-to-Business (P2B) Regulation** survives and
which legacy data acts are repealed into the Data Act; Article 11 sets the **staggered application dates** that
drive every compliance deadline in the package. The P2B question is one of the most contested in the Council.

## Current law
The **P2B Regulation (EU) 2019/1150**, the **Data Governance Act (EU) 2022/868**, the **Free Flow of
Non-Personal Data Regulation (EU) 2018/1807** and the **Open Data Directive (EU) 2019/1024** are all in force
as standalone instruments.

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837)
- **P2B — repealed outright** with effect from *[date = entry into application]*, with a **derogation** keeping
  Art 2(1), 2(2), 2(5), 4, 11 and 15 in force **until 31 December 2032** (legal certainty; cf. recital 59).
- **Repealed:** the **DGA (2022/868)**, the **FFNPD (2018/1807)** and the **Open Data Directive (2019/1024)**,
  their substance relocated principally into the **Data Act (2023/2854)**; references read via the **Annex I
  correlation table**.
- **Art 11:** entry into force on the **third day** after publication; staggered application — ePrivacy Art
  5(3) carve-out at **6 months**; breach-notification routing + cyber build-out (Arts 6(2)–(3), 7–9) at **18
  months**; single-entry-point reporting at **24 months** (where the Commission's Art 23a(7) assessment is
  negative).
- → [Commission text — Arts 10–11](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_final-art10-11.md#article-10-amendments-repeals-transitory)

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **P2B — partially amended, *not* repealed.** Only **Arts 2(11), 2(12), 6, 8–10, 12–14 and 16–18** are
  **deleted**; the rest of the Regulation stays in force. The Commission's **31 Dec 2032 derogation is struck
  out**.
- **Repealed:** DGA, FFNPD and the Open Data Directive (as proposed); correlation via **Annex I** (not part of
  the extract).
- **Art 11:** entry into force on the **third day** after publication; staggered application — machine-readable
  consent / online-interface chapter at **18 months**; ePrivacy Art 5(3) at **6 months**; Art 3 points 8(a)–(c)
  (breach routing) and the cyber build-out (Arts 6(2)–(3), 7–9) at **18 months**; reporting **via the national
  entry point at 30 months** (moved from 24). Bracketed dates remain undecided.

## Parliament — JURI draft opinion (PE789.142, Benifei, 5 Jun 2026)
No joint ITRE/LIBE draft report yet, but the **JURI draft opinion** takes a clear line: **oppose the P2B
repeal.** It deletes the Commission's repeal recital 59
([Am 12](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-12)) and both operative repeal
limbs of Omnibus **Article 10** — the outright repeal of Reg (EU) 2019/1150
([Am 67](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-67)) and the 31 Dec 2032
derogation list ([Am 68](../../extracts/parliament/JURI-PA-789142_draft-opinion.md#amendment-68)). The
justification: the DSA/DMA do not fully replace P2B's fairness/transparency safeguards (ranking
transparency, complaint-handling, unfair-suspension protections) for SMEs outside the DMA's scope. This
aligns JURI with the broad **member-state resistance** to the repeal (below). On the **DGA/Open-Data**
repeals, JURI does not oppose the consolidation as such but **restores the DGA safeguards** the Omnibus
weakened (mandatory registration, data-intermediation neutrality) — see
[Data Act instrument page](../instruments/data-act-2023-2854.md#parliament--juri-draft-opinion).
A committee-for-opinion text, not the draft report; amendment deadline 26 Jun 2026. Via triage
[#47](../triage/2026-06-15-issue-47.md).

<a id="parliament-imco-draft-opinion"></a>
## Parliament — IMCO draft opinion (PE789.877, Agius Saliba, 8 Jun 2026)
The IMCO draft opinion takes the **same line — oppose the P2B repeal** — and, given P2B's internal-market
and consumer-protection subject matter, this sits squarely in IMCO's competence. It deletes the
Commission's repeal recital 59
([Am 25](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-25)) and the operative repeal
limbs of Omnibus **Article 10** — the outright repeal of Reg (EU) 2019/1150
([Am 123](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-123)) and the 31 Dec 2032
derogation list ([Am 124](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-124)), and
removes the consequential deletion of P2B Art 4
([Am 122](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-122)). The rapporteur's
justification (Am 123): *"The full repeal of the P2B, even with a sunset clause, leaves gaps for protecting
business users (SMEs). Instead the Commission should do an impact assessment analysing the exact overlaps
with other laws such as the e-commerce Directive, DSA and DMA rather than repealing it in full in this
omnibus."* This **doubles the committee weight** against the repeal (JURI + IMCO) and aligns with the broad
member-state resistance (below). A committee-for-opinion text, not the draft report; amendment deadline
8 Jul 2026. Via triage [#61](../triage/2026-06-18-issue-61.md).

## EDPB–EDPS
The regulators **welcome merging the Data Acquis into the Data Act** and streamlining overlapping/outdated
rules (Joint Opinion 2/2026, ¶119–121), **but** ask that **DGA safeguards be preserved** through the repeal —
re-insert DGA Art 1(2)/(3), keep data-intermediation and data-altruism safeguards, and keep DGA registration/
oversight (¶129–155). → [JO 2/2026: data intermediation](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#data-intermediation),
[public-sector re-use](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#public-sector-reuse)

## ECB
**ECB (CON/2026/9)** — welcomes the **repeal of outdated instruments**, notably the **FFNPD (2018/1807)**, and
the consolidation of data-sharing rules in the Data Act. → [digest](../advisory/ecb-con-2026-9.md)

## EESC
**EESC (INT/1108)** — supports consolidation; mandatory data-sharing should be limited to where **strictly
necessary**, with a stronger **European Data Innovation Board**. → [digest](../advisory/eesc-int1108.md)

## Status
**P2B is a live battleground.** Most delegations want P2B protections **retained** (several **permanently**),
so the Council's partial-amendment compromise — and *which* articles ultimately survive — is unsettled. The
DGA/FFNPD/Open-Data repeals are broadly accepted. Application dates are still **bracketed**.

## Member-State signals (WK 3736/26)
- **Broad resistance to the P2B repeal.** Coalition **{AT, ES, LV, SE}** wants **Arts 1, 4(2) and 19** kept
  "to maintain coherence … following its partial repeal"; coalition **{BE, CZ, DK, FI, IE, IT, LU, NL, SI}**
  treats certain P2B provisions as **essential SME safeguards** to be kept **permanently** (not just to 2032),
  with **Art 19** and **Art 3(2)** singled out.
- **DE** — wants P2B **Arts 3 and 5 to survive until 31 Dec 2032** to protect SMEs.
- **EE** — the outlier **supporting the clean P2B repeal** (objectives largely covered by the DSA/DMA).
- **BE** — entry into force **on the third day after publication is unfeasible**; needs a transitional period.
- **AT** — notes **eIDAS (Reg 910/2014) is missing from the title**; flags reference corrections.
- → [`../member-state-positions.md`](../member-state-positions.md)

## Open questions
- Which P2B articles ultimately survive — and whether **permanently or until 2032**.
- The final (currently bracketed) **application dates**, and whether the third-day entry into force gains a
  transitional period.
- The content of the **Annex I** correlation table for the repealed acts.
