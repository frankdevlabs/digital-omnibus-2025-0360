# GDPR Art 12(5) & 57 — Manifestly unfounded / excessive / abusive requests

**Tracked because:** narrows data-subject rights by letting controllers (and DPAs) refuse "abusive" requests.

## Current law
Art 12(5): controllers may charge a fee or refuse where requests are "manifestly unfounded or excessive,
in particular because of their repetitive character"; controller bears the burden. Art 57(4): same for DPAs.

## Commission proposal
Adds that a request may be refused where there are **reasonable grounds to believe** it is made for a
purpose **other than** data protection; gives examples of "abuse".

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Retained but reformulated**: for Art 15 requests, refusal where an **"abusive intention" can be
  demonstrated**; controller bears the burden "in the light of all the relevant circumstances".
- Art 57 aligned for DPAs (point (k) of Art 57(1) deleted; para 4 replaced).

## Parliament (provisional)
Likely to tighten further or narrow the "abusive intention" ground.

## EDPB–EDPS
Object to linking "abuse" to the **purpose** of the access request; concerned it chills legitimate access.

## Status
**Contested.** Council has softened the Commission ("intention" + burden on controller) but the provision
survives. noyb's empirical data (83.5% of requests not properly answered) is the centrepiece of opposition.

## Open questions
- Who proves "intention", and to what standard?
- Does "overly broad and undifferentiated" survive in the final recitals?
