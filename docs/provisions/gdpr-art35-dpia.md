# GDPR Art 35 — Data protection impact assessment (lists, template & methodology)

**Tracked because:** moves the DPIA "blacklist/whitelist", template and methodology from **27 national lists**
to **one EU-level set**, and shifts the power to set them from the **Commission** to the **EDPB** — a direct
harmonisation gain for controllers, but a live institutional tug-of-war.

## Current law
Art 35 requires a DPIA for processing likely to result in a high risk. Each **supervisory authority
establishes and publishes its own national list** of operations requiring a DPIA (Art 35(4)) and may publish a
"no DPIA required" list (Art 35(5)); cross-border lists go through the consistency mechanism (Art 35(6)).

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837)
- **Paras 4, 5, 6 replaced:** the **Board (EDPB)** *prepares and transmits to the Commission* proposals for
  (4) the list of operations subject to a DPIA, (5) the list of those not requiring one, and (6) a common
  **template and methodology**.
- **New 6a–6c:** proposals within **[9 months of entry into application]**; the **Commission adopts** them by
  implementing act (Art 93(2)); review at least every three years; existing national lists valid **until the
  Commission adopts** the implementing act.
- → [Commission text](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-9--article-35-dpia-lists)

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Power moved to the EDPB.** Paras 4, 5, 6 replaced so the **Board establishes and makes public** (i) the
  DPIA list, (ii) the no-DPIA list, and (iii) a common **template and methodology** — a **single EU-level**
  set replacing national lists.
- **New 6a–6c:** published within **[9 months]**; the **Commission may adopt the template by implementing
  act**; review at least every three years; existing national DPA lists remain valid **until the Board
  publishes** the EU lists.
- Consequential **EDPB tasks** at **Art 70(1)(ha)/(hb)** — see the
  [EDPB-governance provision](gdpr-edpb-governance.md).
- → [Operative text — point 9](../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-9--article-35-dpia-lists)

## Parliament (provisional)
No draft report yet; EU-level harmonisation of DPIA lists is likely to be welcomed.

## EDPB–EDPS
**Welcomed; EDPB ownership** (Joint Opinion 2/2026, ¶88–95): support common EEA **DPIA lists** and a common
**template + methodology** (¶88, ¶92); as with breach notification, **entrust the EDPB (not the Commission)
with preparing *and approving*** them (¶90, ¶94); the methodology should be a broad, practical **guided
process, not a checklist** (¶93). The parallel amendment to **EUDPR Art 39 is neither necessary nor
appropriate** — the EDPS already adopts EUI DPIA lists — so it **should not be adopted**, and any EUDPR
methodology should be adopted by the **EDPS** (¶91, ¶95). The Council's "EDPB establishes" model **matches
this ask**; the residual Commission implementing-act power over the *template* is the remaining friction.
→ [JO 2/2026: DPIA](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#dpia)

## Status
**Aligned with the EDPB, contested by Poland.** The Council adopted EDPB ownership (what the regulators
wanted); PL pushes the other way, wanting the Commission's implementing-act route restored so the lists bind
all 27 Member States uniformly. The "Commission may adopt the template by implementing act" residue keeps a
hook for that argument.

## Member-State signals (WK 3736/26)
- **PL** — wants the lists to **expressly reflect the risk-based approach** (context, nature, scope, purposes)
  and **does not support** the switch from a Commission implementing act to an "EDPB establishes" model — asks
  to **revert to the Commission proposal** for uniform EU-wide implementation; the lists/template **must not
  replace the individual risk assessment**.
- **RO** — wants **"light DPIA templates"** — simplified assessment models, in particular for SMEs.
- → [`../member-state-positions.md`](../member-state-positions.md)

## Open questions
- **EDPB-establishes vs Commission-adopts** — the core institutional choice (shared with the
  [breach-notification](gdpr-art33-breach-notification.md) template/lists).
- Whether the residual Commission implementing-act power over the *template* survives.
- SME-light templates (RO ask); validity window for national lists during the transition.
