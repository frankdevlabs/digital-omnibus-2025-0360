# GDPR Art 49 / recital 40b — third-country transfers for tax cooperation (FATCA)

**Tracked because:** the Council's **ST 10729/26** negotiating mandate (22 June 2026) adds — late, at
delegations' request and **with no impact assessment** — a derogation pathway through **GDPR Chapter V**
(international transfers) for **periodic/automated transfers of personal data to third countries for tax
cooperation**, i.e. the **FATCA** mechanism. GDPR Chapter V was **untouched by the Commission proposal**, so
this is wholly new scope for the file, introduced at the mandate stage. It intersects the pending CJEU case
**C-804/25** on whether FATCA transfers are compatible with the GDPR.

## Current law
GDPR **Chapter V** (Arts 44–50) governs transfers to third countries. Absent an adequacy decision (Art 45) or
appropriate safeguards (Art 46), **Article 49(1)(d)** permits a transfer "necessary for **important reasons of
public interest**". **Article 49(4)** today provides that that public interest "shall be recognised in Union
law or in the law of the Member State to which the controller is subject." Recital 26 / the "means reasonably
likely to be used" test and the Chapter V safeguards otherwise apply.

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837)
**Silent.** The Commission proposal did **not** amend Chapter V or Article 49. As the Presidency NOTE records
(¶12), the delegations' tax-transfer request "could only be addressed through a possible amendment to Chapter V
of the GDPR … which has not been amended by the Commission proposal", so earlier compromises did not include it.

## Council — ST 10729/26 (22 June 2026) — **new**
Two linked moves (Presidency NOTE ¶¶12–13):

1. **Operative — a "slight amendment" to Article 49** (GDPR extract,
   [point 10a](../../extracts/council/ST-10729-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-10a--article-49-tax-transfers)):
   - **49(1)(d) replaced** → "the transfer is necessary for important reasons of public interest **as recognised
     in Union law or in the law of the Member State to which the controller is subject**";
   - **49(4) deleted** (its content is now folded into point (d)).
   This is largely a **relocation** of the existing Art 49(4) clause into the operative derogation — affirming
   that the public interest must be grounded in Union or Member-State law.

2. **Interpretive — new [recital 40b](../../extracts/council/ST-10729-2026_recitals.md#recital-40b--third-country-tax-transfers)**
   (the substantive payload): "important reasons of public interest" within Art 49(1)(d) **include** "the
   implementation, application and compliance with **international agreements or administrative arrangements on
   mutual assistance or cooperation in tax matters** concluded by a Member State with a third country or an
   international organisation **where personal data are exchanged on a periodic or automated basis**." Member
   States "should ensure that appropriate safeguards are provided"; and where the competent authority finds the
   data subject's rights override the public interest, "the transfer should not take place."

> **Why the recital does the work.** The operative Art 49 edit is modest; the **recital** is what newly
> characterises FATCA-type *periodic/automated* tax-data flows as falling within the Art 49(1)(d) public-interest
> derogation. That is exactly the contested legal question — whether the recurring, bulk transfer of bank data
> to a third-country tax authority can rest on a Chapter V derogation rather than requiring adequacy/appropriate
> safeguards.

**Numbering note:** the Presidency NOTE ¶13 calls it recital **"40a"**, but the Annex operative text numbers it
**(40b)** — (40a) is a separate recital on supervisory-authority consistency.

## Stakeholder reaction — Association of Accidental Americans (Fabien Lehagre)
Surfaced via a **LinkedIn post by Fabien Lehagre** (Association of Accidental Americans), the source through
which this LIMITE mandate reached the tracker. Lehagre argues the Cypriot Presidency **inserted FATCA-enabling
language into the negotiating mandate via an "innocuous-sounding" recital**, without democratic debate or an
impact assessment, and claims it **recycles GDPR-amendment language previously pushed by Spain and rejected**.
The objection: a recital is being used to **pre-empt the very Chapter-V compatibility question** now before the
CJEU, legitimising the automatic annual transfer of "accidental Americans'" bank data to the US IRS. *(The
"recycled Spanish amendment" and "rejected" characterisations are Lehagre's claims; the public record confirms
only that "several delegations" requested the change via the Antici Group — NOTE ¶12. See
[`../stakeholders.md`](../stakeholders.md#association-of-accidental-americans-aaa--fatca).)*

## Live litigation — CJEU C-804/25
The CJEU registered **Case C-804/25** (lodged 10 Dec 2025, accepted 26 Feb 2026), putting ~13 questions on
**FATCA's compatibility with the GDPR and the Charter**: Articles **45, 46 and 49** on cross-border transfers;
**5(1)(b)** purpose limitation, **5(1)(c)** data minimisation, **5(1)(e)** storage limitation; **12–14**
transparency; and **35** (DPIA). Several national DPAs (notably **Belgium**) have already found FATCA transfers
breach the GDPR. By codifying — at recital level — that Art 49(1)(d) "includes" periodic/automated tax-cooperation
transfers, the Council mandate would bolster the **Member-State/Council reading** that such transfers have a valid
Chapter V basis, cutting against the complainants' case.

## Status
**New and contested.** Added in ST 10729/26 (22 June 2026), to be **confirmed at Coreper-II on 26 June 2026** as
part of the trilogue negotiating mandate (not yet a Council act). No impact assessment; the EDPB–EDPS were not
consulted on this specific addition (their Joint Opinion 2/2026 predates it). Watch whether it survives Coreper,
the Parliament's reaction, and any interaction with the C-804/25 proceedings.

## Open questions
- Can an **interpretive recital** validly expand the reach of an Art 49(1)(d) **derogation** (which the CJEU
  reads narrowly), or does it overstep what a recital may do?
- What "appropriate safeguards" would Member States actually provide for **bulk, automated** FATCA transfers,
  and who polices the "rights override → no transfer" carve-out?
- How does the addition interact with **C-804/25** — does it pre-empt the Court, or merely restate the
  Member-State position the Court is reviewing?
- Why was a Chapter V change introduced **at the mandate stage with no impact assessment** and no EDPB–EDPS
  opinion, given the NL "clean cut" position that fundamental-rights GDPR changes belong in a separate process?
- Is there **reciprocity** (the US does not provide equivalent data to EU tax authorities), and does that bear
  on the proportionality of the public-interest justification?
