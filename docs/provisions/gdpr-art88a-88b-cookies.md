# Cookies / terminal-equipment consent — GDPR Art 88a/88b & ePrivacy Art 5(3)

**Tracked because:** decides **where** the cookie-consent regime lives (GDPR vs ePrivacy), the
consent-exempt purposes, the anti-consent-fatigue rule, and machine-readable consent signals.

## Current law
ePrivacy Directive **Art 5(3)**: storing/accessing information on terminal equipment needs consent, save
strict-necessity exceptions. In NL: Telecommunicatiewet **Art 11.7a**.

## Commission proposal
- **GDPR Art 88a (new):** moves the consent rule into the GDPR; lists consent-exempt purposes
  (transmission; explicitly requested service; first-party audience measurement; security); easy single-
  click refusal; **6-month no-repeat** after refusal.
- **GDPR Art 88b (new):** controllers must honour **automated, machine-readable** consent/refusal/withdrawal
  signals; browser/OS providers must offer the technical means; media-service exemption.

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **GDPR Art 88a: DELETED.** The consent rule is **kept in the ePrivacy Directive (amended Art 5(3))**,
  carrying the exempt-purpose list, single-click refusal and the **6-month** no-repeat rule, and
  designating the GDPR supervisory authority as enforcer. (Aligns with Poland's position, WK 3736/26.)
- **GDPR Art 88b: RETAINED** (machine-readable signals), renumbered after the deletion of 88a
  (numbering unsettled in the source — verify). Browser/OS technical-means duty kept; staged application
  (signals after 24 months; browser/OS duty after 48 months).

## Parliament (provisional)
Highly contested. Industry pushes deletion of the machine-readable-signal article; digital-rights side
wants broader OS/app coverage and stronger signal-honouring.

## EDPB–EDPS
Support effective machine-readable consent and oppose consent fatigue; cautious on scope.

## Status
**Major battle, but reframed.** The conceptual fight ("cookies into GDPR") is — for now — resolved by the
Council keeping cookies in ePrivacy. The live issue is the **machine-readable signal** article and its
browser/OS obligations.

## Open questions
- Final article number for the machine-readable-signal provision.
- Scope: web browsers only, or also operating systems / in-app?
- Interaction with the future ePrivacy reform and with the IAB TCF.
