# GDPR Art 88c — Legitimate interest for AI development/operation

**Tracked because:** would have created an explicit statutory legitimate-interest basis (Art 6(1)(f)) for
training and operating AI on personal data.

## Current law
No AI-specific legal basis. AI training on personal data must satisfy ordinary Art 6 (often 6(1)(f) with
the three-part balancing test), per EDPB **Opinion 28/2024**.

## Commission proposal
- **GDPR Art 88c (new):** AI development/operation "may be pursued for legitimate interests within the
  meaning of Article 6(1)(f)", save where Union/national law requires consent or where overridden by data-
  subject rights (esp. children); with data-minimisation, anti-leakage safeguards, transparency, and an
  **unconditional right to object**.
- Supporting recitals 30 and 31 (benefit to society; balancing factors).

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Art 88c: DELETED.** Recitals **30 and 31 also deleted.**
- The AI Art 6(1)(f) reading survives **only in recital 33a** (interpretive, not operative): AI
  development/deployment **may** be a legitimate interest, except where consent is legally required or
  rights override (esp. children), with safeguards (data minimisation, transparency, right to object).
- → [Recital 33a operative text](../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_recitals.md#recital-33a-ai-legitimate-interest)

## Parliament (provisional)
EDRi/noyb push for full deletion of any AI LI statement; EPP/Renew more supportive of a recital. Kaljurand
cautious.

## EDPB–EDPS
A standalone article is **unnecessary** — Opinion 28/2024 already frames AI legitimate interest.

## Status
**Major battle.** The Council removed the operative article but kept the interpretive recital; the
Parliament may seek to delete even the recital, or conversely industry may seek to re-elevate it to an
article. Net practical effect today: rely on **ordinary Art 6(1)(f) + Opinion 28/2024**, not on Art 88c.

## Member-State signals (WK 3736/26)
- **RO** — proposes to **eliminate or clarify "regurgitation"** (recital 31): the term is undefined and
  not used in the operative provisions.
- **PL** — "previous comments remain valid" on recitals 30/31.
