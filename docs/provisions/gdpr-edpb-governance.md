# GDPR EDPB tasks, consistency & DPO contact — Arts 64, 70, 37(7)

**Tracked because:** these are the "plumbing" amendments that decide **what the EDPB does** under the new
regime — it gains the power to *establish* the DPIA and breach instruments and to issue the pseudonymisation
opinion — plus a small **DPO-contact** simplification. They are where the Commission-vs-EDPB ownership fight
(see [DPIA](gdpr-art35-dpia.md) and [breach notification](gdpr-art33-breach-notification.md)) is wired in.

## Current law
- **Art 64** — EDPB opinions and the consistency mechanism (the Board opines on draft national measures and
  acts referred to it).
- **Art 70** — the EDPB's tasks (guidelines, recommendations, best practices, encouraging codes, etc.).
- **Art 37(7)** — the controller/processor **publishes the DPO's contact details and communicates them to the
  supervisory authority**.

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837)
- **Points 12–14:** **Art 64(1)(a) deleted**; **Art 70(1)(h) deleted**; new Art 70(1) points **(ha)** DPIA
  lists, **(hb)** DPIA template/methodology, **(hc)** breach-notification template + high-risk list — each
  framed as the EDPB **"prepares and transmits a proposal"** (for Commission adoption).
- **Art 37(7):** *no change proposed* (the Art 37(7) amendment below is a Council insertion).
- → [Commission text — points 12–14](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#points-1214--articles-64--70-edpb-tasks-and-consistency)

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Point 9a — Art 37(7) replaced:** *"The controller or the processor shall publish the contact details of
  the data protection officer."* — i.e. the duty to **communicate them to the supervisory authority is
  dropped** (a simplification).
- **Points 12–14 — Arts 64 & 70:**
  - **Art 64(1)(a) deleted**;
  - **Art 70(1)(f) amended** — guidelines on the criteria for **profiling** decisions under Art 22(1);
  - **Art 70(1)(h) deleted**;
  - new Art 70(1) points: **(ha)** *establish* the DPIA lists; **(hb)** *establish* the DPIA template/
    methodology; **(hc)** *establish* the breach-notification template + high-risk lists; **(hca)** issue
    **guidelines on Art 32 security** measures; **(hcb)** issue the **pseudonymisation/anonymisation opinion
    under Art 29a**.
  - ▸ Note the shift from the Commission's *"prepare and transmit a proposal"* to the Council's
    ***"establish"*** — the substantive transfer of power to the EDPB.

## Parliament — IMCO draft opinion (PE789.877, Agius Saliba, 8 Jun 2026)
No joint ITRE/LIBE draft report yet, but the **IMCO draft opinion** reinforces EDPB/DPA governance and
extends enforcement to browser/software providers. (a) The EDPB makes the templates/lists **public** and the
Commission **"shall take utmost account of the Board's assessment"**
([Am 108](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-108),
[Am 109](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-109)). (b) A **new Art 58(6a)**
extends DPA investigative and corrective powers to **"providers of software to access online interfaces"**
(e.g. browsers) in the context of Art 88b even when they are not processing personal data
([Am 112](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-112)). (c) Amendments to **Art
83** add non-compliance with Arts 88a/88b as a fineable infringement and extend the fine provisions to
software providers ([Am 113](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-113),
[Am 114](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-114)). **JURI** is silent here —
its draft opinion is data-acquis-only. A committee-for-opinion text, not the draft report; amendment deadline
8 Jul 2026. Via triage [#61](../triage/2026-06-18-issue-61.md).

## EDPB–EDPS
Cross-cutting recommendation #2 (Joint Opinion 2/2026): **entrust the EDPB (and the EDPS for the EUDPR) with
both preparing *and approving*** the breach template/high-risk lists and the DPIA lists/template/methodology —
**not the Commission** (¶82–84, ¶90–95). The Council's "EDPB **establishes**" wording, plus the new Art 32
security-guidance task and the Art 29a opinion, **reflect that ask**. The regulators also stress adequate
**EDPB/SA resourcing** for the expanded mandate.
→ [JO 2/2026: breach notification](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#breach-notification),
[DPIA](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#dpia)

## Status
**EDPB-empowering, with a live counter-current.** The Council strengthened the EDPB's role (aligned with the
regulators); Poland pushes back, preferring Commission implementing acts for the lists/templates the EDPB now
"establishes" (see [DPIA](gdpr-art35-dpia.md) and [breach notification](gdpr-art33-breach-notification.md)).
The Art 37(7) simplification is uncontroversial.

## Member-State signals (WK 3736/26)
- **FR** — a governance cluster: add an **Art 32(5)** EDPB security-guidance mandate (partly met by the new
  Art 70(1)(hca)); have **national SAs refrain** from issuing guidance on matters already covered by the EDPB;
  and **extend the Art 64/65 consistency mechanism so that controllers and processors can themselves ask the
  EDPB for an opinion** on cross-border matters.
- **PL** — generally wants the **Commission implementing-act route kept** for the lists/templates the EDPB now
  establishes (so they bind all 27 Member States).
- → [`../member-state-positions.md`](../member-state-positions.md)

## Open questions
- Whether controllers/processors get **standing to request EDPB opinions** (FR ask).
- **EDPB-establishes vs Commission-adopts** — unresolved across the DPIA and breach instruments.
- Resourcing of the EDPB's enlarged task list (security guidance, pseudonymisation opinion, lists/templates).
