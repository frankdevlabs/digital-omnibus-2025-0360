# GDPR pseudonymisation & data protection by design — new Art 29a, Art 25

**Tracked because:** this is where the deleted Art 4(1) personal-data redefinition (see the
[Art 4 provision](gdpr-art4-personal-data.md)) re-surfaces as **process**: a new Article on
pseudonymisation/identifiability and an **EDPB opinion** mechanism, plus a tweak extending the
**data-protection-by-design** duty to processors. It decides *who* sets the criteria for when pseudonymised
data effectively prevents re-identification — the Commission, or the EDPB.

## Current law
- No Article 29a. Pseudonymisation is defined in **Art 4(5)** and rewarded across **Arts 6(4)(e), 25, 32,
  89**; identifiability is assessed by **"all the means reasonably likely to be used"** (Recital 26).
- **Art 25** — data protection **by design and by default** binds **the controller**.

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837)
- **New Art 41a:** the **Commission** *may adopt implementing acts* specifying means/criteria for when data
  resulting from pseudonymisation **no longer constitute personal data for certain entities**; the Commission
  assesses the state of the art and develops re-identification-risk criteria; the EDPB gets an **8-week
  opinion** on the draft; adoption via the examination procedure (Art 93(3)).
- **Art 25:** *no change proposed* (the Art 25 amendment below is a Council insertion).
- Recitals 27a/27b on pseudonymisation and "singling out".
- → [Commission text — Art 41a](../../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-10--article-29a-pseudonymisation)

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Art 41a deleted; replaced by new Article 29a** (EDPB-centred):
  - (1) controllers/processors **may apply pseudonymisation** to reduce risk and help meet obligations;
  - (1a) identifiability assessed by **all means reasonably likely to be used, such as singling out**, by the
    controller **or another person**;
  - (2) the **Board issues an opinion** under **Art 64(2)** on pseudonymisation/anonymisation (means and
    criteria for when pseudonymisation may effectively prevent persons *other than the controller* from
    identifying a data subject);
  - (3) the Chair requests the opinion within **12 months of entry into force**; reviewed/updated as needed.
  The Commission's implementing-act paragraphs are **struck out**.
- **Art 25 (new point 7a):** paragraphs 1–2 replaced so the obligations bind **"the controller and the
  processor"** (not the controller alone), with explicit reference to the **Art 35(4)/(5)** lists; substance
  otherwise as today.
- A consequential **EDPB task** is added at **Art 70(1)(hcb)** (issue the Art 29a pseudonymisation/
  anonymisation opinion) — see the [EDPB-governance provision](gdpr-edpb-governance.md).
- → [Operative text — point 10](../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-10--article-29a-pseudonymisation),
  [point 7a (Art 25)](../../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-7a--article-25-data-protection-by-design-and-by-default)

## EDPB–EDPS
**Pseudonymisation by implementing act (Art 41a) — opposed** (Joint Opinion 2/2026, ¶22–25): delineating
personal data **defines the material scope** of EU data-protection law, which must rest with **independent
supervisory authorities under judicial control** (Art 8(3) Charter) and the EDPB's consistency role — **not
the Commission** → **delete Art 41a**. Open questions are **better handled by forthcoming EDPB
pseudonymisation/anonymisation guidance** than by amending the definition (¶19). Cross-cutting: **keep
scope-defining rules in primary law, not implementing acts**. The Council's switch to an EDPB-opinion model
**moves in the direction the regulators urged**.
→ [JO 2/2026: pseudonymisation by implementing act](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#implementing-act-pseudonymisation)

## EESC
**EESC (INT/1108)** — by contrast, **supports Commission implementing acts** clarifying when pseudonymised
data may be treated as anonymised for other entities, as important for AI training while keeping privacy
safeguards (i.e. closer to the Commission's original Art 41a route). → [digest](../advisory/eesc-int1108.md)

## Status
**Re-cast, not settled.** The Council's EDPB-opinion mechanism aligns with the EDPB–EDPS; but the *form* is
contested from two sides — PL wants the Commission's Art 41a implementing power kept, and FR wants the EDPB
output to be a **binding opinion**, not soft guidance.

## Member-State signals (WK 3736/26)
- **FR** — wants the **EDPB to adopt a binding _opinion_ under Art 64(3)** (not non-binding guidelines), with
  a deadline, so controllers get concrete enforceable identifiability/pseudonymisation criteria fast.
- **PL** — **does not support deleting Art 41a**; warns recital 27a wording ("…may no longer constitute
  personal data for certain entities") could **destabilise Art 4(1)**; guidance should be framed **"without
  affecting the definition of personal data in Article 4(1)"** and consistent with Recital 26.
- **RO** — supports the "means reasonably likely to be used" criteria as **reducing SME uncertainty**;
  proportionate, simplified SME pathways.
- **DE** — insists the **SRB judgment / "relative anonymity"** be properly reflected.
- → [`../member-state-positions.md`](../member-state-positions.md)

## Open questions

- Interaction with the deleted Art 4(1) redefinition — see the [Art 4 provision](gdpr-art4-personal-data.md).
- Whether extending Art 25 to processors creates new free-standing processor obligations.
