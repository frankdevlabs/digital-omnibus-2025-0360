# GDPR research, secondary use & transparency exemptions — Arts 4(38), 5(1)(b), 13(4)–(5)

**Tracked because:** this is the "research-friendly GDPR" package — a statutory **definition of scientific
research**, a **purpose-limitation** rule that makes research/archiving/statistics secondary use compatible
by default, and two **transparency derogations** (low-risk relationships; research). Operationally decisive
for anyone reusing personal data for analytics, product development or research.

## Current law
- **Art 5(1)(b)** — purpose limitation: data collected for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes; further
  processing for archiving/scientific or historical research/statistics is *not* incompatible, **subject to
  Art 89(1)** safeguards.
- **No statutory definition** of "scientific research" (only Recital 159).
- **Art 13(4)** — the duty to inform does not apply where the data subject already has the information.
- **Art 13** has **no research-specific derogation** for data collected *from* the data subject (the
  impossibility/disproportionate-effort derogation today sits in Art 14(5)(b), for indirectly-collected data).

## Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837)
- **Art 4(38) (new):** defines "scientific research" as research that can also support innovation, contributes
  to/applies scientific knowledge, aims at society's general knowledge and wellbeing, and adheres to ethical
  standards — expressly **"does not exclude that the research may also aim to further a commercial interest."**
- **Art 5(1)(b) replaced:** research/archiving/statistics further processing compatible **in accordance with
  Art 89(1)**, **independent of the conditions of Art 6(4)**.
- **Art 13(4) replaced:** exemption where data collected in a **"clear and circumscribed relationship"** and
  the activity is **"not data-intensive"** and there are reasonable grounds the subject already has the Art
  13(1)(a)/(c) info — with carve-outs (other recipients, third-country transfer, Art 22(1) ADM, Art 35 high
  risk).
- **Art 13(5) (new):** no duty to inform where research processing makes it impossible/disproportionate
  (subject to Art 89(1)); appropriate safeguards incl. making information publicly available.

## Council — ST 9547/26
- **Art 4(38): re-included and reworked.** Earlier reporting had it deleted in the February compromise; the
  May text carries a redrafted definition — research conducted **"in an autonomous and independent manner"**,
  following a **methodological and systematic** approach consistent with the field's standards (incl. ethical
  standards), producing **verifiable and transparent** results. The explicit "may also aim to further a
  commercial interest" sentence is **dropped** into the cleaner formulation.
- **Art 5(1)(b): retained** — research/archiving/statistics further processing compatible **subject to
  appropriate safeguards in accordance with Art 89(1)**, **independent of Art 6(4)**.
- **Art 13(4): retained, reworked** — exemption now keyed to a **"direct and clearly circumscribed
  relationship"** and an activity **"not likely to result in a high risk"** (replacing "not data-intensive"),
  with explicit carve-outs for complex operations, large-scale processing, special-category / criminal data,
  other purposes/recipients, third-country transfer, Art 22(1) ADM and Art 35 high-risk processing.
- **Art 13(5): retained** — the research derogation now applies where the further processing is **"by the
  same controller"**.

## Parliament (provisional)
No operative committee amendment touches the research definition or the Art 13 transparency rules: the JURI
(PE789.142) and IMCO (PE789.877) committees-for-opinion tabled none, and the lead joint ITRE/LIBE draft report
is pending (Oeil: "Awaiting committee decision"). The only committee touch is a recital-level signal — IMCO
recital 44 tightens the terminal-equipment carve-out to "strictly necessary **and proportionate**" for a
service "**explicitly** requested" ([Am 21](../../extracts/parliament/IMCO-PA-789877_draft-opinion.md#amendment-21)),
which points toward a tighter reading of low-risk processing but does not amend Art 4(38) or Art 13. The
substance therefore remains open pending the joint report.

## EDPB–EDPS
**Scientific research — welcomed, with improvements** (Joint Opinion 2/2026, ¶26–35): welcome the new
definition and the clarification that research further processing is compatible **independent of Art 6(4)**
(¶30); but move the *methodological/systematic, autonomous, verifiable, transparent* criteria **into the
enacting terms**, and move the "innovation / commercial interest" phrasing **out of the definition into
recitals** (context, not qualifying criteria — risks excluding humanities/social science) (¶29); extend the
**Art 13(5)** derogation to data originally collected *from* the data subject via "where and insofar" wording
(¶33). **Transparency derogation (Art 13(4)) — improve** (¶60–64): clarify "**not data-intensive**" and
"**clear and circumscribed relationship**"; **drop "reasonable grounds to assume"**; require the controller to
**still provide full Art 13 info on request** and to say so; **align EUDPR Art 15(4)**.

## EESC
**EESC (INT/1108)** — the research definition should keep its **commonly accepted meaning** (per EDPB and CJEU
case-law) while enabling industrial research, and warns against broad/absolute limitations of Charter Arts 7–8;
supports SME-oriented templates and examples. → [digest](../advisory/eesc-int1108.md)

## Status
**Broadly supported simplification.** The contested points are drafting-level: whether the research criteria
live in recitals or the enacting terms, how tightly "not high risk" / "clearly circumscribed relationship" is
bounded, and whether the Art 13(5) derogation reaches directly-collected data.

## Member-State signals (WK 3736/26)
- **Scientific research** — **FR** wants explicit **independence** safeguards ("free from undue pressure");
  **PL** **opposes deleting** Art 4(38), insists processing **primarily for commercial product development must
  not bypass Art 89 safeguards**, and that further processing stays tied to **Art 89(1)**; **RO** wants the
  regime to expressly cover **SME innovation / applied research / product development**.
- **Transparency exemptions** — **FR supports** the Art 13 changes; **PL** welcomes the more objective wording
  but wants the exemption kept **strictly limited to genuinely simple, low-risk situations** and the Art 13(5)
  derogation **exceptional and case-by-case**, consistent with Art 5(1)(a) and Art 89; **DE** backs simplifying
  Art 13/14 information duties.

## Open questions
- Recital vs enacting-terms placement of the research criteria (EDPB ask).
- Whether the Art 13(5) research derogation extends to data collected directly from the data subject.
- The boundary of "not likely to result in a high risk" / "clearly circumscribed relationship" for Art 13(4).
