# Stakeholders

Positions of regulators, civil society and industry. Links resolve via [`../sources/README.md`](../sources/README.md).

## Regulators

- **EDPB & EDPS — Joint Opinion 2/2026 (10 Feb 2026).** Support simplification and competitiveness in
  principle, but **strongly urge co-legislators not to adopt** the personal-data redefinition (out of
  line with CJEU case law) and reject the standalone AI legitimate-interest article as unnecessary.
  Criticise the abuse-of-rights provision for linking abuse to the *purpose* of the request.
  **Welcome** the high-risk/96-hour breach-notification threshold, the EU-wide DPIA list, the
  biometric-verification derogation, and the single-entry point (with safeguards).
- **ECB — Opinion (10 Mar 2026).** Financial-stability-relevant aspects (DORA single-entry point,
  abuse-of-rights).
- **EESC — Opinion (18 Mar 2026).**

## Civil society

- **noyb (Max Schrems).** Calls it "the biggest attack on Europeans' digital rights in years."
  Multi-version legal analysis (V3, 24 Feb 2026); "reality check" of 16 Apr 2026 found **83.5% of
  access requests** in its case-set were not answered in line with the law — used to rebut the
  "abusive requests" rationale.
- **EDRi.** After the February compromise: *"A step back from the brink, the risks remain"* — welcomes
  deletion of the Art 4 redefinition, the research definition and the pseudonymisation implementing
  power; continues to oppose the AI legitimate-interest provision and the sensitive-data carve-out.
- **BEUC.** The proposal "goes far beyond targeted modifications" and weakens consumer protections.
- **127 organisations** signed an open letter (13 Nov 2025) urging the Commission to drop the GDPR
  reopening. **Corporate Europe Observatory / LobbyControl** mapped Big-Tech lobbying behind specific
  provisions (Jan 2026).

## Industry

- **CCIA, DOT Europe, Bitkom, AI Chamber** — open letter to the Council (May 2026) arguing the Cyprus
  compromise did **not go far enough** on simplification: restore the Art 4 personal-data
  clarification and **delete the machine-readable-consent article** ("consent chaos"). Note the
  Commission's own estimate that the single-entry point "would require 8 FTEs within ENISA" — seen as
  insufficient for 24/7 cover across 27 Member States and 24 languages.

## Parliament-side controversy

- Civil-society **call to withdraw Aura Salla** as rapporteur (Corporate Europe Observatory,
  Transparency International EU and others, Feb 2026) over her former role as Meta's EU public-policy
  lead and a non-declaration of the potential conflict of interest. ITRE coordinators did not withdraw
  the appointment.
