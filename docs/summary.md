# Summary

The **Digital Omnibus** (data/cyber strand, file **2025/0360 (COD)**, Commission proposal
**COM(2025) 837 final** of 19 November 2025) is the Commission's "simplicity by design" package
amending the **GDPR**, the **ePrivacy Directive**, the **NIS2 Directive**, **DORA**, the **CER
Directive** and **eIDAS**, and repealing or amending the **P2B Regulation**, the **Data Governance
Act**, the **Free Flow of Non-Personal Data Regulation** and the **Open Data Directive**. Its stated
goals are competitiveness (the Draghi agenda) and an administrative-burden cut of at least 25%
(35% for SMEs).

It is **not** the AI-Act Digital Omnibus (`2025/0359`), which is a separate, faster file.

## What it would change (Commission proposal)

- **GDPR.** A relative/controller-centric clarification of when data is "personal"; a new scientific-
  research definition and broadened research/AI legitimate-interest bases; a derogation for
  incidental special-category data in AI training and a biometric-verification derogation; the power
  to refuse "manifestly unfounded or excessive or abusive" data-subject requests; extended
  transparency exemptions; a rewrite of Article 22 (automated decisions); data-protection-by-design
  extended to processors; a breach-notification threshold limited to high-risk breaches with a
  72-hour deadline; a single EU-level DPIA list; and a relocation of the cookie/terminal-equipment
  consent rules from ePrivacy into the GDPR, with a 6-month "no-repeat" rule and machine-readable
  consent signals.
- **Cyber.** A single-entry point for incident reporting (ENISA-operated information point + national
  entry points), so that GDPR, NIS2, DORA, CER and eIDAS reports can be filed once.
- **Data acquis.** Consolidation into the Data Act; repeal of several instruments.

## Where it actually stands (29 May 2026)

- **Council:** third Cyprus Presidency compromise text (**ST 9547/26**, 21 May 2026); exchange of
  views at the AGS on 27 May; **no general approach**; file expected to pass to the Irish Presidency.
- **Parliament:** committee referral only — [Oeil](https://oeil.europarl.europa.eu/oeil/en/procedure-file?reference=2025/0360(COD))
  status *Awaiting committee decision*; joint **ITRE + LIBE** (Rule 59), **IMCO** & **JURI** opinions;
  **no draft report yet**.
- **EDPB–EDPS:** Joint Opinion 2/2026 (10 Feb 2026) opposes the core GDPR changes ([digest](advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md)).
- **ECB & EESC:** ECB Opinion CON/2026/9 (10 Mar 2026) is broadly supportive but asks to **carve DORA
  out of the single-entry point** ([digest](advisory/ecb-con-2026-9.md)); EESC opinion INT/1108
  (18 Mar 2026) backs simplification "as a first step" provided standards are not lowered
  ([digest](advisory/eesc-int1108.md)).
- **Member States:** delegation comments from FR, PL, RO, DE, EE, CZ, AT and DK (plus two coalitions
  resisting the P2B repeal) are summarised in [`member-state-positions.md`](member-state-positions.md).

## The headline of the May Council text

The Council has **walked back** the most contested Commission ideas: it **deleted** the personal-data
redefinition, the AI legitimate-interest article (Art 88c), and the article moving cookies into the
GDPR (Art 88a) — keeping the cookie regime in an **amended ePrivacy Directive** instead — and largely
**restored Article 22** to its current form. It **kept** the breach-notification reform, the EU-level
DPIA list (run by the EDPB rather than the Commission), the single-entry point, the machine-readable
consent signals, and a **reworked** scientific-research definition. See
[`institutional-positions.md`](institutional-positions.md) and [`fault-lines.md`](fault-lines.md).
