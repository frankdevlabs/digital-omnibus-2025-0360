## What was flagged

A new, today-dated Dutch government letter to the Tweede Kamer, **"Naderende Raadspositie Omnibus
Digitaal"** (Approaching Council position on the Digital Omnibus), id `2026Z13570` / `2026D30585`,
dated **18 June 2026**, with an accompanying Beslisnota (`2026D30586`). Signed by the **State Secretary
for Economic Affairs (W.J.M. Aerdts)** and the **State Secretary for Justice & Security (Claudia van
Bruggen)**, also on behalf of the Ministers of J&V and Interior (BZK). The tracker read the in-scope
phrase *Omnibus Digitaal* but the operative content needed a manual read.

Confirmed by downloading and reading the DOCX (`tweedekamer.nl/downloads/document?id=2026D30585`). The
letter is a substantive negotiation-status report. Key contents:

- **Procedural headline:** the **Cypriot Presidency intends, at the Coreper-II meeting of 26 June 2026,
  to present the compromise it expects to have reached by then and to request a mandate to negotiate
  with the European Parliament (trilogue).** The Council negotiations are "in an concluding phase"
  (*afrondende fase*). The government stresses the *krachtenveld* is still very much in motion and the
  compromise can still change on important points; no definitive conclusions yet.
- **Impact assessment:** the cabinet rejects the Commission's "no significant effects" reading for the
  Omnibus Digitaal, has repeatedly asked for an impact assessment, but lacks Council support to force
  one; cites the Commission's **28 April 2026 Better Regulation communication** course-change (more
  impact assessments in future, including for simplification/omnibus proposals).
- **Cyber single reporting point:** a majority of Member States appears to support — in line with the
  NL non-paper — **retaining national reporting structures plus a European central information point**,
  and an obligation on Member States to set up a national central reporting point; NL wants flexibility
  to fit existing national structures.
- **P2B:** enough Member States appear to support keeping certain P2B provisions (SME protection).
- **Data legislation (Data Act strand):** cabinet largely positive on the original proposal; the Council
  compromise likely will not fundamentally deviate; insufficient support to delete the data-altruism and
  emergency-data-request provisions, but support to simplify/clarify them; ongoing discussion on the
  trade-secret refusal-of-data-sharing amendments (NL wants the duty to justify and notify the regulator
  retained).
- **GDPR/AVG — NL tabled text amendments in the Council** on: the personal-data definition (Art 4(1)) /
  pseudonymisation (delete); scientific-research definition (delete or significantly amend; supports
  EDPB/EDPS wording — autonomous/independent, methodological, verifiable/transparent results); residual
  special-category data in AI (delete; fallback: limit to "technically impossible", drop the
  "disproportionate effort" route); abusive access requests Art 12(5) (supports EDPB/EDPS "abusive
  intention"); automated decision-making Art 22 (supports EDPB/EDPS proposal *not* to amend it as the
  Commission proposed); Commission empowerments (keep EDPB competent on pseudonymisation/DPIA/breach
  lists); AI legitimate interest Art 88c (delete — and oppose its return even as a recital); plus
  technical points (e.g. Art 33(6) — also a list of circumstances where breach notification is *not*
  required). NL reports several of these "find resonance" in the Council, helped by the EDPB/EDPS Joint
  Opinion 2/2026.

(The letter also has an *Akkoord Omnibus AI* section, but only because the Kamer asked for a combined
addendum to the AI- and Digital-Omnibus fiches; the negotiation substance is all Omnibus Digitaal.)

## Assessment

**Substantive and in-scope (2025/0360).** The interinstitutional subject is unambiguous: GDPR/AVG,
ePrivacy/cookies, cyber incident-reporting (single reporting point), P2B and the Data Act strand — the
data/cyber Omnibus (COM(2025) 837), **not** the AI-Act file 2025/0359. The BNC-fiche it refers to is the
12 December 2025 *Fiche Omnibus Digitaal*.

The single most newsworthy item is procedural: **NL reports the Cypriot Presidency aiming for a Coreper-II
negotiating mandate on 26 June 2026** — i.e. a late push to fix the Council's first-reading position
*before* the 1 July handover to Ireland. This updates the repo's prior "no general approach before H2
2026 / file passes unclosed to the Irish Presidency" framing (which rested on the 9 June Telecom Council).
It must be recorded with the proper hedge: this is a **Member-State government's report of Council
dynamics**, not a confirmed Council act, and the letter itself warns the compromise can still change.

The GDPR substance largely **updates and operationalises the existing NL line** (the 8 April non-paper):
positions are now framed as concrete text-amendment proposals tabled in the Council, with NL's read of
where each is landing. It does **not** change the operative text of any version, so the `what-changed.md`
diff table is untouched.
