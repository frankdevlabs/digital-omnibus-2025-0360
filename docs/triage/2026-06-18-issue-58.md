## Assessment
**Substantive and in-scope — log it.**

Fetched the live page (2026-06-18). The leaflet is an educational/advocacy piece, not a new
legal analysis, but it carries a clear and noteworthy position:

- It is about the **data/cyber Digital Omnibus (2025/0360)** ePrivacy/cookies strand —
  in-scope (not the AI file 2025/0359). EDRi says the proposal "has reopened an important
  debate about how privacy rights should actually work online" amid the current "maze of
  banners, settings menus, and consent requests".
- It frames **machine-readable communication of privacy preferences** — i.e. the
  **Article 88b** signal mechanism — *positively*, as a path "beyond today's endless consent
  clicks" toward "rights respected by design". This is a notable, non-obvious nuance: EDRi
  (civil society) **welcomes** the very article that **industry** (CCIA/DOT Europe/Bitkom/AI
  Chamber) calls "consent chaos" and wants deleted, and that **France** (WK 3736/26) also
  wants deleted. EDRi's general stance on the package remains critical ("a step back from the
  brink, the risks remain"); this leaflet is narrower and supportive *on the Art 88b concept
  specifically*.

The leaflet does **not** flag a concern that machine-readable consent could be weakened or
misused — so this entry should record EDRi's framing as supportive of the *concept*, without
over-claiming an endorsement of the whole proposal.
