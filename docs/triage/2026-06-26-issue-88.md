## Assessment

**In scope and substantive.** The cover page carries interinstitutional reference
**2025/0360(COD)** and **COM(2025)0837 – C10-0303/2025**, amending GDPR 2016/679, EUDPR 2018/1725,
Data Act 2023/2854, ePrivacy 2002/58, NIS2 2022/2555 and CER 2022/2557, and repealing FFNPD 2018/1807,
P2B 2019/1150, DGA 2022/868 and Open Data 2019/1024 — i.e. the **data/cyber** Omnibus, **not** the AI
Act file 2025/0359. Co-rapporteurs Salla (ITRE) + Kaljurand (LIBE) match the repo's recorded joint
committee responsible. Scope confirmed.

The operative text **was retrievable at triage** via the shared headless-browser AWS-WAF bypass
(`fetch_blocked_doc.py`; doceo returns HTTP 202 to curl/WebFetch) — so this is **not** a metadata-only
/ `pending_operative_text` case. PDF committed under `sources/parliament/`.

**Thrust of the 78 amendments** — the co-rapporteurs substantially **walk back the omnibus
deregulation**:

- **P2B restored** — Am 77–78 delete the repeal of Reg (EU) 2019/1150; Am 12 deletes the repeal recital 59.
- **GDPR Art 22 restored** — Am 51 reinstates the *right not to be subject* to solely automated decisions;
  Am 53 defines *meaningful* human intervention (reviewer with authority/knowledge/competence to modify).
- **Subject-access "abuse" reframing reversed** — Am 5 (recital 35), Am 48–49 (Art 12(5)): data subject
  presumed to act for reasonable purposes; "choice between" a fee or refusal; controller bears the burden.
- **Scientific-research / further-processing kept but re-anchored to Art 89 safeguards** — Am 2, 3, 45.
- **Biometric verification tightened** — Am 4, 46, 70: necessity + proportionality, sole control, Union-law safeguards.
- **EDPB (not the Commission) authors the breach-notification template and the DPIA lists/templates** —
  Am 7, 8, 56–67; Commission "may" adopt by implementing act.
- **Breach-notification deadline stays 96 hours** — Am 54 (does *not* roll back to the original 72h).
- **NIS2 'report-once' / harmonised application** — Am 11 (recital 49a), Am 75 (new Art 14a), Am 76
  (Art 23a(3a) common single-entry-point template).
- **Data Act strand strengthened** — Am 13–42: intermediation-service safeguards (no advertising/profiling/
  ranking/AI-training use, separate legal person, mandatory registration), B2G emergency narrowing
  ("strictly necessary" pseudonymisation), third-country-access transparency reports, open-data exclusivity/
  charges limits, GDPR-SA cooperation and a strengthened EDIB.

This **partly refutes** the earlier *unconfirmed* Bluesky/LinkedIn report (J. Tar, 12 Jun) that LIBE
amendments would *strip* the AI-training, pseudonymisation and research-exemption provisions: the joint
report **conditions and tightens** those provisions rather than deleting them outright (and Am 25
positively bars intermediation data being used for AI training). Update the open STATUS watch line accordingly.
