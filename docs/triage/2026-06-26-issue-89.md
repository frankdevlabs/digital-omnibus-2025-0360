**Confirmed from the source (`ah-1255667.pdf`, 5 pp.):** *Aanhangsel* **AH 10** (Eerste Kamer, vergaderjaar 2025-2026) — answers to written questions by **lid Van de Sanden** (announced 2 Jun 2026), answered **23 June 2026** by the **minister van Buitenlandse Zaken mede namens de minister van Economische Zaken en Klimaat**, "over de inzet van de omnibusprocedure en het ontbreken van effectbeoordelingen." Ten numbered Q&A. Key substance:

- **A1** — BZ holds the coordinating role for EU affairs; **EZK** owns the better-regulation policy line on whether omnibus procedures carry impact assessments.
- **A2/A9** — Cabinet welcomes simplification; does **not** object to substantive changes in an omnibus *provided* they fit the simplification objective and underlying policy aims hold; but considers it **undesirable that impactful proposals are presented without an impact assessment**, and will keep pressing the Commission to do one (or convincingly justify its absence).
- **A3** — **None of the ten omnibus packages published since 26 February 2025 carried a Commission impact assessment.**
- **A4** — Since the **Antici Group on Simplification first discussed the AI + Digital omnibus on 21 Nov 2025**, the cabinet has repeatedly pressed for an impact assessment — orally and in written comments, and via the **8 Apr 2026 GDPR non-paper** (`NL-NONPAPER-2026-04-08`) — and sought like-minded Member States, but there is **insufficient Council support** to compel one. On outcomes: the **Omnibus Digitaal *krachtenveld* is still developing**, so no conclusion on the result of the NL effort yet — but **"positieve signalen"** on the cabinet's main concerns.
- **A5** — Aware of the **Danish Folketing** EU-committee letter of **8 May 2026** to Dombrovskis (only 14 of 64 key proposals had an IA); shares the analysis.
- **A6** — Shares the **EDPB–EDPS Joint Opinion 2/2026** regret that a full impact assessment is missing and fundamental-rights effects under-weighed.
- **A7** — Reads the Commission's **COM(2026) 380** ("A Simpler, Clearer and Better Enforced EU Rulebook") as reaffirming that simplification/omnibus proposals should have impact assessments and that the Commission will do more of them.
- **A8** — Legal view: using an omnibus procedure **without** an IA is **not in itself unlawful**; settled CJEU case law imposes no obligation to do an IA in all circumstances (C‑482/17 *Czech Republic v Parliament and Council*; C‑541/20 a.o. *Lithuania v Parliament and Council*); no ground to treat a resulting regulation as void.
- **A10** — Sees no added value in seeking a **Raad van State** advisory opinion. Q10 notes the aim to **conclude the Council Digital Omnibus negotiations by end June 2026**.
