## What was flagged

A new Dutch government letter to the Tweede Kamer, **"Voortgang onderhandelingen Omnibus Digitaal en nadere
informatie over bepaalde aspecten van online tracking"** (kamerstuk `2026Z15870` / `2026D35677`, **6 July
2026**), from Staatssecretaris **W.J.M. Aerdts** (Economische Zaken en Klimaat) and Staatssecretaris **K.T.
"Claudia" van Bruggen** (Justitie en Veiligheid), mede namens de minister van BZK. The tracker matched the
Dutch-scope phrase "Omnibus Digitaal".

Confirmed by fetching the letter (DOCX at `tweedekamer.nl/downloads/document?id=2026D35677`, committed as
[`NL-KAMERBRIEF-2026-07-06`](../../sources/README.md#member-state-positions)). Two strands:

1. **Council negotiations.** The **Cypriot Presidency ultimately decided _not_ to ask the Council, at
   Coreper II on 26 June 2026, for a mandate to open trilogues** on the Omnibus Digitaal. In the days before
   Coreper a **blocking minority** of Member States had formed; part of it found the compromise showed
   **insufficient "ambition" on simplification, in particular on the GDPR/AVG** (i.e. wanted *more*
   deregulation). The Dutch cabinet had judged the compromise sufficient as a negotiating mandate. Support
   appears to persist on **P2B, the cyber incident-reporting point and data legislation**; but a **growing
   group wants further-reaching, as-yet-unknown GDPR changes**, so Council support for the GDPR strand in
   line with the NL inzet is **uncertain**. Negotiations **continue under the Irish Presidency** (planning
   not yet clear); NL keeps working to align the compromise with its BNC-fiche inzet.
2. **Online tracking (Rathenau).** Answering a 25 June Commissiedebat commitment (questions by leden
   **Kathmann** (PRO) and **Swinkels** (CDA) on the Rathenau report *"De prijs van gratis internet"*):
   minors already enjoy EU protection; the contextual-advertising option = a de-facto full ban on
   ad-personalisation with wide effects on free services/journalism/SMEs, so NL prefers to **streamline** the
   EU online-tracking rules (less consent fatigue / dark patterns), incl. in the Omnibus; national measures
   are very limited, so a **wetsvoorstel is in preparation** to concentrate cookie/online-tracking
   supervision at the **Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP)** (structural funds since 2024).
