## What was flagged
A watched EU data-protection commentator posted a meme-format quote-post (*"From: the CJE / To: Digital
Omnibus"*) tying a fresh **CJEU** judgment on the scope of "health data" to the Digital Omnibus. The
quoted context: the Court "states that the information published is not covered by the concept of 'health
data', unless the publication refers to the name or category of the prohibited method or substance
involved."

Confirmed source: the judgment is **Case C-474/24**, delivered **14 July 2026** (four Austrian athletes,
AR/YT/DI/RN, sanctioned under anti-doping proceedings). Holding relevant here: the mere fact that a person
violated an anti-doping rule and was suspended does **not, in and of itself, constitute health data**
within the meaning of **Article 9 GDPR**; that changes only where the publication names the **prohibited
substance or method**, which — with other information — can directly or indirectly reveal the athlete's
state of health. The Court also held that mandatory online publication of an athlete's name/duration/reason
can comply with the GDPR provided the controller can conduct a case-by-case balancing before publishing.
(Verified via two independent write-ups of the ruling; the Bluesky app is JS-rendered and returns no
server-side text.)
