## Assessment

moving through the Council and European Parliament" — expressly distinct from the already-adopted AI
Omnibus (2025/0359). Both are genuine civil-society **position pieces**, not pure commentary → they
belong in `docs/stakeholders.md` (and the EDRi/cookies fault-line).

Confirmed positions (post 1): opposes **narrowing the personal-data definition** (esp. pseudonymised
data), **weakening special-category protections** and **cutting transparency rights**; opposes moving
**device-access rules "away from the ePrivacy logic of confidentiality"**; backs a **binding privacy
signal** (a single browser/device opt-out) over cookie banners — i.e. reiterates its support for the
Art 88b-style machine-readable signal; attacks the missing impact assessment as "SPF 0 law-making".
Post 2 (Fitness Check): the rights-based framework (GDPR/DSA/DMA) is a competitive advantage and is
"fit for purpose"; the EU should **strengthen enforcement and close gaps** (ban spyware, regulate
ad-tech) rather than simplify/reopen.

The posts' **procedural** claims — Council stalled before recess, a **DE/PL blocking minority**,
Parliament (LIBE/ITRE) preparing positions, an EP-requested substitute impact assessment — are
**already recorded** in `STATUS.md` (via the 6 Jul NL government Kamerbrief, [#105](2026-07-09-issue-105.md)).
No procedural fields change; EDRi's blog only corroborates them from a secondary source. Cf. #58 (EDRi
