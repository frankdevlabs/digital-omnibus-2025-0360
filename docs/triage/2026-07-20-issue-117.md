## What was flagged

A new EDPB feed item dated **17 July 2026** reporting a **high-level EDPB meeting in Dublin on
16–17 July 2026**, at which the Board "called for a clear legal basis for the sharing of information
among regulators with different competences" and **calls upon the European Commission to propose a
legal basis for cross-regulatory information sharing**, enabling regulators to exchange information,
"including confidential information", relevant to enforcement in their respective areas of competence.

The tracker itself noted that the statement **does not name the Digital Omnibus**, so relevance to
2025/0360 needed a manual check.

**Confirmed against the source** (item page fetched in full,
[`edpb-calls-for-legal-basis-for-cross-regulatory-information-sharing_en`](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/news/edpb-calls-for-legal-basis-for-cross-regulatory-information-sharing_en)).
The item has two strands:

1. **Cross-regulatory cooperation** — the call on the Commission for a legal basis for information
   sharing between regulators with adjacent competences; quote from Chair **Anu Talus** on "the need
   for stronger legislation to facilitate more effective cross-regulatory cooperation".
2. **GDPR enforcement capacity / consistency** — rising complaint volumes (AI-driven), stretched DPA
   resources, pooling of resources and joint operations, workshops on enforcement procedures "also in
   the context of implementing the upcoming **Procedural Regulation**", and a stock-take of the **2025
   Helsinki Statement** follow-up.

Neither strand mentions COM(2025) 837, 2025/0360, the Digital Omnibus, the GDPR amendments, the cyber
single-entry point, NIS2/DORA/CER, or the Data Act.

## Assessment

**In scope thematically, but not a position on this file — contextual only; a narrow, hedged note is
warranted, no per-provision or `positions.csv` change.**

- **It is not an act on 2025/0360.** No procedural step, no advisory output on the proposal, no new
  EDPB position on any tracked provision. The ask is for a **future, separate** Commission proposal,
  and the enforcement-capacity strand belongs to the **GDPR Procedural Regulation** (file 2023/0202),
  not this one. So it is *not* the 10 June 2026 plenary case (where the Omnibus was "among the key
  topics" and the Board restated its Art 4 opposition) — that one earned a `TIMELINE.md` row; this
  one does not.
- **But it directly re-asserts an existing JO 2/2026 recommendation on this file.** In Joint Opinion
  2/2026 **¶160–162** the EDPB and the EDPS asked the co-legislators to add an **explicit legal basis
  for cross-regulatory information exchange** between Data Act competent authorities and GDPR
  supervisory authorities — one of the opinion's five cross-cutting recommendations ("effective
  enforcement … cross-regulatory information exchange"), already digested at
  [`advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md`](../advisory/edpb-edps-jo-2-2026.md#data-enforcement). The Dublin
  statement generalises that same ask to the Commission at large. That makes it a **sourced signal that
  the recommendation is live and escalating**, worth recording where the recommendation is digested —
  provided the note says plainly that the statement does not name the Omnibus.
- **Not the cyber strand.** The tracker's lead ("same policy space as Art 6–9, single incident-reporting
  entry point") is only loosely right: the omnibus cyber strand is about **entity-to-authority incident
  reporting** funnelled through a single entry point, whereas the Dublin call is about
  **authority-to-authority enforcement information exchange between DPAs and other digital regulators**.
  No claim about [`provisions/cyber-single-entry-point.md`](../provisions/cyber-single-entry-point.md)
  is supportable from this source.
