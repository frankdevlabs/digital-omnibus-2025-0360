Operative wording, verbatim from the mirrored Council text:

> **Simplification** — The Council heard a presentation by the presidency and held an exchange of
> views on the state of play and future orientation of work on the EU's simplification agenda. The
> Irish presidency intends to follow up on the task given by the Leaders through the 'One Europe,
> One Market' Roadmap, striving to close the Omnibus packages VII to X (on digital policy,
> environment, automotive, and food and feed safety, respectively) by the end of the year, assuming
> the European Parliament is ready to negotiate swiftly, and beginning the work on the two new
> packages on taxation and energy products, respectively.

**"Omnibus simplification legislative packages, progress report, 12 June 2026"**. Ministers discussed
how to sustain simplification momentum "without undermining EU policy goals".

## Assessment

**Substantive, in scope, and Council-level — not stakeholder commentary.**

- **Scope:** "Omnibus package VII" is the Council's own label for the **Digital Omnibus**; the repo
  already uses it (CM 3630/26 agenda item 1 "Omnibus VII: Digital Omnibus"; the withdrawn 26 Jun
  agenda item 46 "Omnibus VII (Digital omnibus)"). Not the AI-Act file 2025/0359 (Omnibus VIII in
  Council counting, adopted 29 Jun 2026). In scope.
- **Novelty:** this is the **first Council-level statement of a target adoption timeline** for this
  file. Until now the repo could only record that the Cyprus mandate was never sought and that the
  Irish Presidency had taken the file back to working-party level (CM 3630/26, 16 Jul Antici) —
  "planning unclear" per the 6 Jul NL Kamerbrief. The GAC statement supplies the Presidency's
  intended end-point: **close by end-2026**, i.e. a trilogue deal inside H2 2026.
- **Weight — an intention, not a commitment.** It is a Presidency statement of intent at a public
  GAC session, expressly **conditional on the EP negotiating swiftly**, and covering four omnibus
  packages generically rather than this file specifically. It also sits in tension with the file's
  own state of play: the Council has **no mandate**, is back at **working-party level** (16 Jul
  Antici exchange of views on a discussion note), and the EP's lead committees only closed
  amendment-tabling on the joint ITRE/LIBE draft report on **15 Jul**, with the committee vote
  expected **~Q4 2026** — so trilogues could not realistically start before Q4. Record it as a
  **timing signal with its condition attached**, not as a scheduled milestone.
- **Layer:** procedural reportage of a Council session, not a position document. Analysis layer only
  — cite by URL, no `data/documents.yaml` entry, no `sources/` commit. (The Council's own *progress
  report on omnibus packages, 12 June 2026* would be a registrable document, but it is not what was
  flagged and it covers all packages — noted below as a watch item.)
