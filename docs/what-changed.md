# What changed — full provision-by-provision diff

The comprehensive companion to [`STATUS.md`](../STATUS.md)'s "What changed" summary. It is the **authority on
what moved**: for every tracked provision it traces **current law → Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837, 19 Nov
2025) → latest Council text (ST 9547/26, 21 May 2026)**, so it is clear *which institution changed what*.

Several widely-reported "features of the proposal" were in fact deleted, moved or already in the base text —
this page is the myth-buster. The most common confusion is **Art 33**: the **96-hour deadline and high-risk-only
threshold were already in the Commission proposal**, not Council changes (the Council changed only the *routing*
and *timeline*).

**How to read each row.** The **provision** cell links to its analysis page in [`provisions/`](provisions/);
each substantive cell ends with a deep link into the operative text — the **Commission** column into
[`extracts/commission/`](../extracts/commission/), the **Council** column into
[`extracts/council/`](../extracts/council/). Provision-by-provision institutional positions are in
[`data/positions.csv`](../data/positions.csv); working transcriptions — **verify against the authoritative
source before relying on them**.

## GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679)

| Provision | Current law → Commission proposal (COM(2025) 837, Nov 2025) | Latest Council text (ST 9547/26, May 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| [**Personal data (Art 4(1))**](provisions/gdpr-art4-personal-data.md) | *Current:* "personal data" = information on an identified/identifiable person, identifiability judged by "all the means reasonably likely to be used" (Recital 26). **Commission** adds a relative/controller-centric clause — data is **not** personal for an entity that cannot itself identify the person, and does not become personal merely because a later recipient could. [Commission pt 1](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-1--article-4-definitions) | **Addition deleted** (point 1(a) struck) — Art 4(1) reverts to current law; the Commission's Art 41a implementing power is also dropped, replaced by a new Art 29a EDPB-opinion route. Likely cross-institutional consensus *not* to redefine. [ST 9547/26 pt 1](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-1--article-4-definitions) |
| [**Scientific research (Art 4(38))**](provisions/gdpr-research-secondary-use.md) | *Current:* no statutory definition (only Recital 159). **Commission** adds Art 4(38) defining "scientific research", expressly stating it "does not exclude that the research may also aim to further a commercial interest." [Commission pt 1](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-1--article-4-definitions) | **Deleted in Feb (ST 6406), re-included & tightened in May** — research must be conducted "in an autonomous and independent manner", methodically and systematically, producing verifiable/transparent results; the explicit commercial-interest sentence is dropped. [ST 9547/26 pt 1](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-1--article-4-definitions) |
| [**Special categories in AI (Art 9(2)(k)/9(5))**](provisions/gdpr-art9-special-categories-ai.md) | *Current:* Art 9(1) bars special-category processing save the exhaustive Art 9(2)(a)–(j) derogations. **Commission** adds Art 9(2)(k) for incidental/residual special-category data in AI development/operation, under new Art 9(5) safeguards. [Commission pt 3](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-3--article-9-special-categories) | **Retained, tightened** — a sequenced duty (avoid collection → erase if detected → only if erasure is impossible/disproportionate apply technical/organisational protection: no further processing, outputs or disclosure), plus regular verification + documentation across the AI life cycle. [ST 9547/26 pt 3](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-3--article-9-special-categories) |
| [**Biometric verification (Art 9(2)(l))**](provisions/gdpr-art9-special-categories-ai.md) | *Current:* no dedicated derogation for one-to-one biometric verification. **Commission** adds Art 9(2)(l) for biometric verification where the data and means are under the data subject's **sole control**. [Commission pt 3](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-3--article-9-special-categories) | **Retained with safeguards** — "one-to-one verification", means under sole control, plus a Union/Member-State legal-basis hook (Art 9(4)). [ST 9547/26 pt 3](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-3--article-9-special-categories) |
| [**Abusive requests (Art 12(5) & 57)**](provisions/gdpr-art12-57-abusive-requests.md) | *Current:* controllers/DPAs may charge or refuse "manifestly unfounded or excessive" requests, burden on the controller. **Commission** adds refusal where there are "reasonable grounds to believe" a request is made for a purpose **other than** data protection. [Commission pt 4](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-4--article-12-5-abusive-requests) | **Retained but narrowed** — refusal of Art 15 requests only where an "abusive intention" can be **demonstrated** by the controller "in the light of all the relevant circumstances"; Art 57 aligned for DPAs. [ST 9547/26 pt 4](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-4--article-12-5-abusive-requests) |
| [**Transparency exemptions (Art 13(4)/(5))**](provisions/gdpr-research-secondary-use.md) | *Current:* Art 13(4) drops the duty to inform only where the subject already has the info; no research-specific derogation for directly-collected data. **Commission** exempts a "clear and circumscribed", "not data-intensive" relationship and adds a new research derogation (Art 13(5)). [Commission pt 5](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-5--article-13-4-information-obligations) | **Retained, reworked** — exemption keyed to a "direct and clearly circumscribed relationship" and an activity "not likely to result in a high risk" (replacing "not data-intensive"), with explicit carve-outs; the Art 13(5) research derogation kept for same-controller processing. [ST 9547/26 pt 5](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-5--article-13-4-information-obligations) |
| [**Automated decisions (Art 22)**](provisions/gdpr-art22-automated-decisions.md) | *Current:* a right not to be subject to solely-automated decisions, save (a) contract, (b) law, (c) explicit consent. **Commission** rewrites Art 22 to a more permissive form and adds that (a) applies "regardless of whether the decision could be taken otherwise than by solely automated means" (Recital 38). [Commission pt 7](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-7--article-22-automated-decisions) | **Largely restored to current GDPR** — the right-based framing kept; the "regardless of whether…" clause and supporting Recital 38 deleted. De-escalated. [ST 9547/26 pt 7](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-7--article-22-automated-decisions) |
| [**Pseudonymisation & by-design (Art 29a, Art 25)**](provisions/gdpr-pseudonymisation-by-design.md) | *Current:* no Art 29a; identifiability judged by "all means reasonably likely to be used"; Art 25 by-design binds the controller. **Commission** adds Art 41a giving the **Commission** implementing power to set when pseudonymised data is no longer personal for certain entities (8-week EDPB opinion). [Commission pt 10](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-10--article-29a-pseudonymisation) | **Art 41a deleted, replaced by new Art 29a** — an **EDPB opinion** (Art 64(2)) sets the pseudonymisation/anonymisation criteria; Art 25 by-design extended to bind "the controller **and the processor**". [ST 9547/26 pt 10](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-10--article-29a-pseudonymisation) |
| [**Breach notification (Art 33)**](provisions/gdpr-art33-breach-notification.md) | *Current GDPR:* notify the DPA within **72h** unless the breach is "unlikely to result in a risk". **The Commission already moves to 96h + a high-risk-only threshold**, routed through the EU single-entry point (Art 23a NIS2). [Commission pt 8](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-8--article-33-breach-notification) | **96h / high-risk-only kept** (a Commission change, **not** the Council's). Council switches routing to the **national entry point** (Art 23b), adds an interim direct-notification fallback (new Art 33(1a)), and extends the switch-over **24 → 30 months**; the EDPB establishes the template + high-risk lists. [ST 9547/26 pt 8](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-8--article-33-breach-notification) |
| [**DPIA lists (Art 35)**](provisions/gdpr-art35-dpia.md) | *Current:* each DPA sets and publishes its **own national** DPIA "blacklist/whitelist". **Commission** moves these to one EU-level set, with the **EDPB** proposing and the **Commission adopting** them by implementing act. [Commission pt 9](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-9--article-35-dpia-lists) | **Power moved to the EDPB** — the Board "establishes and makes public" the DPIA lists + a common template/methodology (a single EU set); the Commission may still adopt the *template* by implementing act. [ST 9547/26 pt 9](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-9--article-35-dpia-lists) |
| [**EDPB tasks, consistency & DPO contact (Arts 64, 70, 37(7))**](provisions/gdpr-edpb-governance.md) | *Current:* Art 64 consistency mechanism; Art 70 EDPB tasks; Art 37(7) requires publishing the DPO's contact details **and** communicating them to the DPA. **Commission** has the EDPB "prepare and transmit a proposal" for the DPIA/breach instruments (for Commission adoption). [Commission pts 12–14](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#points-1214--articles-64--70-edpb-tasks-and-consistency) | **EDPB-empowering** — wording shifts to the EDPB "**establishes**" the lists/templates; new tasks (Art 32 security guidance; the Art 29a pseudonymisation opinion); and Art 37(7) is simplified (the duty to notify the DPA of the DPO is dropped). [ST 9547/26 pts 12–14](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#points-1214--articles-64--70-edpb-tasks-and-consistency) |
| [**Machine-readable consent signals (Art 88b)**](provisions/gdpr-art88a-88b-cookies.md) | *Current:* no such duty. **Commission** adds Art 88b: controllers must honour automated, machine-readable consent/refusal/withdrawal signals, and browser/OS providers must offer the technical means (media-service exemption). [Commission pt 15](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent) | **Retained** (renumbered after Art 88a's deletion; final number unsettled) — browser/OS technical-means duty kept, staged (signals after 24 months; browser/OS duty after 48 months). Live battleground. [ST 9547/26 pt 15](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent) |
| [**AI legitimate interest (Art 88c)**](provisions/gdpr-art88c-ai-legitimate-interest.md) | *Current:* no AI-specific basis (ordinary Art 6(1)(f) balancing + EDPB Opinion 28/2024). **Commission** adds a standalone Art 88c making AI development/operation a legitimate interest, plus recitals 30 & 31. [Commission pt 15](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent) | **Deleted** — recitals 30 & 31 also deleted; the reading survives only in **interpretive recital 33a**. Net effect today: rely on ordinary Art 6(1)(f) + Opinion 28/2024, not on Art 88c. [ST 9547/26 recital 33a](../extracts/council/ST-9547-2026_recitals.md#recital-33a-ai-legitimate-interest) |

## Cyber incident-reporting (NIS2 et al.)

|---|---|---|
| [**Single-entry point for incident reporting**](provisions/cyber-single-entry-point.md) | *Current:* separate incident-reporting channels and deadlines under NIS2 (Art 23), DORA (Art 19), CER (Art 15), eIDAS and GDPR (Art 33). **Commission** adds NIS2 Arts 23a–23d: an ENISA information point + Member-State national entry points ("report once, share many"); application 18 months (extendable to 24). [Commission Art 6](../extracts/commission/COM-2025-837_cyber-art6-9.md#article-6--amendments-to-the-nis2-directive-eu-20222555) | **Retained** — ENISA's information point (Art 23a) *identifies* applicable obligations and redirects (it stores nothing); **Member States** build interoperable national entry points (Art 23b) that actually receive reports; reporting switch-over **24 → 30 months**.
