This is the **incident-reporting single-reporting-point** architecture. The single mechanism threads
through NIS2 (this Article 6), eIDAS, DORA and CER (Articles 7–9), and — handled by another worker —
the GDPR via inserted **Article 23b** referenced from GDPR Art 33(1) and **Recital 49**. See
[provision page](../../docs/provisions/cyber-single-entry-point.md) and
[NIS2 instrument page](../../docs/instruments/nis2-2022-2555.md).

▸ **Major restructuring vs ST 9547/26.** The mechanism is no longer a single ENISA-run "single-entry
point" through which notifications physically flow. ST 10729/26 splits it into (i) an **ENISA incident
reporting information point** that only *informs and directs* (it cannot receive, store or process any
notification), and (ii) **Member-State-run national entry points** (new Art 23b) that actually receive
the reports, configured flexibly per national structures. Per the Presidency NOTE ¶11(iii),
**Article 23b(1)** was further adjusted to allow flexibility in *establishing* the national entry point
(and Recital 49 updated accordingly). The whole "report once, fulfil all obligations" single-platform
build-out, the ENISA piloting/Commission-notice machinery and the Art 23a(7) corrective-measures
fallback that existed in earlier drafts are **struck out**.

## Article 6 — Amendments to the NIS2 Directive (EU) 2022/2555

<a id="article-23a-information-point"></a>
### New Article 23a — Incident reporting information point (ENISA)

**(1)** ENISA shall develop and maintain an **incident reporting information point** to support the
identification and fulfilment of the obligation to report incidents and related events under the Union
legal acts where those Union legal acts provide so ("incident reporting information point").

**(2)** The incident reporting information point shall:
- *(a)* be designed to allow, on the basis of relevant information provided, the identification of the
  applicable reporting obligations referred to in paragraph 1 and the **direction to the appropriate
  national entry point referred [to] in Article 23b**; and
- *(c)* make available simplified and documented information on incident notification processes in the
  different Member States.

**(3)** When developing the incident reporting information point, ENISA shall consult the relevant
national competent authorities under the relevant Union legal acts, the Cooperation Group, the CSIRT
Network and other relevant bodies or groups established at Union level. ENISA shall establish structured
communication channels to ensure that the information available on the incident reporting information
point is swiftly and effectively updated. Member States shall communicate to ENISA all relevant and
necessary information for the purpose of paragraph 1a.

**(4)** The incident reporting information point **shall not enable the submission, transmission, storage
or processing of any incident notification or related data, and shall not collect any information
allowing the identification of the notifying entity or of any incident.**

**(5)** After establishing the incident reporting information point and in cooperation with the
Cooperation Group, ENISA shall explore the possibility to extend the information point by providing a
report on:
- *(a)* regulatory mapping of relevant EU legal acts imposing cybersecurity risk-management measures;
- *(b)* national measures, including transposition measures, implementing relevant Union legal acts
  imposing cybersecurity risk-management obligations;
- *(c)* content to support entities in complying with obligations, in particular regarding entity
  registration and cybersecurity risk-management.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: the ENISA component is **demoted from a notification conduit to an information/redirection
service**. The express prohibition on receiving, storing or processing notifications (para 4) is new, as is
the "explore extension" report (para 5). [Paragraph numbering in the source is irregular — points appear in
the order (1), (2), (3), (5), (4); transcribed as numbered in the source.]

<a id="article-23b-national-entry-point"></a>
### New Article 23b — National entry point for incident reporting (Member States)

**(1)** Member States shall **establish and maintain a national entry point** for supporting and
facilitating the reporting of incidents and related events under the Union legal acts where those Union
legal acts provide so.

**(2)** Member States shall **retain flexibility to configure their national entry point** in accordance
with their existing national structures and the allocation of competences among authorities, including by
connecting existing systems and enabling the sharing, routing or distribution of relevant information.

**(3)** Member States shall **endeavour to design** their national entry point with a view to making the
national entry points **interoperable** with the national entry points of other Member States.

**(4)** ENISA shall, by **[12 months from the date of entry into force of this amending Regulation]**, in
consultation with the CSIRTs network, the Cooperation Group and other relevant bodies, provide
**non-binding recommendations** to support Member States in the establishment, maintenance and secure
operation of their respective national entry point. Those recommendations shall address technical,
operational and organisational measures, taking into account experiences and lessons learned from existing
reporting structures, and shall include technical specifications to ensure interoperability between all
Member States' national entry points (paragraph 3) and to facilitate the alignment of incident
notifications with cross-border reporting obligations.

**(5)** ENISA shall, by **[18 months from the date of entry into force of this amending Regulation]**, in
cooperation with Member States, **develop an open-source solution** that may be used by Member States in
setting up their national entry point under paragraph 1, with the objective of strengthening
interoperability between national systems and enabling faster, more effective cross-border data sharing
when a situation requires coordinated action.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: Art 23b is recast. The earlier text obliged Member States to "designate or establish one
or more national entry points" that were themselves interoperable conduits; ST 10729/26 makes establishment
mandatory but **flexibility in configuration explicit** (para 2 — per NOTE ¶11(iii)) and downgrades the
interoperability duty to *endeavour* (para 3), backed by ENISA **non-binding recommendations** (para 4) and
an **open-source solution** (para 5) rather than binding specifications.

<a id="article-23c-harmonising-framework"></a>
### New Article 23c — Harmonising incident notification framework

**(1)** By **[6 months after the entry into force of this Regulation]** the Commission shall submit a
report to the European Parliament and the Council outlining **common elements and differences in
definitions, thresholds, deadlines, formats and procedures** applying to Article 23 of Directive (EU)
2022/2555, Article 19a(1a), Article 24(2a) and Article 45a(3a) of Regulation (EU) 910/2014, Article 33(1)
of Regulation (EU) 2016/679, Article 19(1) and (2) of Regulation (EU) 2022/2554, and Article 15(1) of
Directive (EU) 2022/2557. The report shall in particular consider concrete steps and a timeline for
introducing a **unified approach** to incident reporting.

**(3)** Building on that report, ENISA shall, in cooperation with the CSIRTs network, the Cooperation
Group, competent authorities and other relevant bodies, **develop guidelines for further harmonisation**
on incident notifications. These guidelines shall in particular:
- *(a)* identify ways to further harmonise templates for entities and between CSIRTs across different
  sectors and legislative frameworks;
- *(b)* provide a thorough analysis of the different sectoral thresholds and, where appropriate, suggest
  possible harmonisation to improve efficiency;
- *(c)* review the stages of incident notifications under different Union legal acts and recommend measures
  to streamline the process for entities.

ENISA shall present to the Commission and Council a **first draft within 12 months** of entry into force,
a **final version within 18 months**, and shall update them regularly thereafter.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: the "common framework / report once, share many" implementing-act mandate is replaced by a
softer **Commission report → ENISA guidelines** route. [Source numbering jumps (1) → (3); transcribed as in
the source.]

<a id="article-23d-cross-border"></a>
### New Article 23d — Cross-border incident notification

**(1)** Member States **should aim at facilitating cross-border notifications** between national entry
points where incidents have cross-border impacts.

**(2)** ENISA shall, in cooperation with the CSIRTs network, the Cooperation Group, competent authorities
and other relevant bodies, **harmonise its relevant tools** with national incident-notification templates
and **facilitate an automatisation process** to exchange information about cross-border impacts.

**(3)** Member States shall **retain control over the scope of information shared** and any access to such
information.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: the earlier mandatory "the national entry point *shall ensure* the notification is shared
with all relevant authorities" is softened to a Member-State *aim* plus ENISA tooling, with an express
Member-State control over information scope.

<a id="article-23-significant-incidents"></a>
### Article 23 (significant incidents) — amended

**(a)** In paragraph 1, the first sentence is replaced so that essential and important entities notify,
without undue delay, their CSIRT or, where applicable, competent authority of any incident with a
significant impact (significant incident) **via the national entry point pursuant to Article 23b**.

A new **paragraph 1b** is added: ENISA shall, in cooperation with the Cooperation Group, **provide a
mapping** of all the competent authorities and CSIRTs referred to in paragraph 1 in the **incident
reporting information point established pursuant to Article 23a**.

**(b)** A new **paragraph 12** is added: where a manufacturer notifies a severe incident pursuant to
**Article 14(3) of Regulation (EU) 2024/2847** (Cyber Resilience Act) and that reporting contains the
information required under paragraph 4 of this Article, the manufacturer's CRA report **shall constitute**
reporting of information under paragraph 4 of this Article.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: the channel changes from "Article 23a" to **Article 23b** (consistent with the split). The
paragraph 1b ENISA-mapping duty and the paragraph 12 **CRA single-reporting bridge** (Art 14(3) of Reg
2024/2847) are carried in this section.

<a id="article-30-voluntary"></a>
### Article 30(1) — voluntary notification — amended

Member States shall ensure that, in addition to the Article 23 obligation, notifications can be submitted
to the CSIRTs or, where applicable, the competent authorities, **on a voluntary basis via the national
entry point referred to in Article 23b**, by:
- *(a)* essential and important entities — incidents, cyber threats and near misses;
- *(b)* entities other than those in point (a), regardless of whether they fall within the Directive's
  scope — significant incidents, cyber threats and near misses.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: substance unchanged; the cross-reference moves from "Article 23a" to **Article 23b**.

## Article 7 — Amendments to the eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014

- **Article 19a — new paragraph 1a:** notifications pursuant to paragraph 1, point (b), to the supervisory
  body and, where applicable, other competent authorities, shall be made **through the national entry point
  pursuant to Article 23b of Directive (EU) 2022/2555**.
- **Article 24 — new paragraph 2a:** notifications pursuant to paragraph 2, point (fb), to the supervisory
  body and, where applicable, other competent bodies, shall be made **through the national entry point
  pursuant to Article 23b** NIS2.
- **Article 45a — new paragraph 3a:** notifications pursuant to paragraph 3 to the Commission and the
  competent supervisory body shall be made **through the national entry point pursuant to Article 23b** NIS2.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: substance unchanged; the channel reference shifts from "single-entry point / Article 23a"
to **national entry point / Article 23b**.

## Article 8 — Amendments to DORA, Regulation (EU) 2022/2554

- **Article 19(1), first subparagraph (replaced):** financial entities shall report **major ICT-related
  incidents** to the relevant competent authority referred to in Article 46 **via the national entry point
  established pursuant to Article 23b of Directive (EU) 2022/2555**, in accordance with Article 19(4).
- **Article 19(2), first subparagraph (replaced):** financial entities may, **on a voluntary basis, notify
  via the national entry point** (Art 23b NIS2) **significant cyber threats** to the relevant competent
  authority where they deem the threat relevant to the financial system, service users or clients; the
  competent authority may pass such information to other relevant authorities (paragraph 6).

▸ vs ST 9547/26: substance unchanged; channel reference shifts from "single-entry point / Article 23a" to
**national entry point / Article 23b**. As before, DORA's own (stricter) timelines and templates continue
to apply — the change is the **channel**, not the substance. No DORA-specific *carve-out* is added in this
version; the carve-out is the GDPR-side derogation in the application-dates clause (see
[final provisions](ST-10729-2026_final-art10-11.md)).

## Article 9 — Amendments to the CER Directive (EU) 2022/2557

- **Article 15(1), first sentence (replaced):** Member States shall ensure that critical entities notify
  **via the national entry point established pursuant to Article 23b of Directive (EU) 2022/2555** the
  competent authority, without undue delay, of incidents that significantly disrupt or have the potential
  to significantly disrupt the provision of essential services.
- **Article 15(2) — new subparagraph:** the **Commission may adopt implementing acts** further specifying
  the type and format of information notified pursuant to Article 15(1), in accordance with the examination
  procedure referred to in Article 24(2).

▸ vs ST 9547/26: substance unchanged; channel reference shifts from "single-entry point / Article 23a" to
**national entry point / Article 23b**. The implementing-act power (format/procedure) is retained.

---

**Application dates** (see [final provisions](ST-10729-2026_final-art10-11.md)): the institutional
build-out — Articles 6(2)–(3), 7, 8 and 9 — applies **18 months** after entry into force; the obligations
to report **via the national entry point** apply **30 months** after entry into force. Until the national
entry point exists, entities notify the competent authority/supervisory authority directly.
