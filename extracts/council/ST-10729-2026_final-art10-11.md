## Article 10 — Amendments, repeals and transitory clauses

**1. Platform-to-Business Regulation (EU) 2019/1150 — amended (not repealed).**
The following provisions are **deleted as of [date of entry into force]**: Articles **2(11)**, **2(12)**,
**6**, **8 to 10**, **12 to 14**, **16**, **17**, and **18 paragraphs (2) to (4)**.
▸ vs ST 9547/26: the partial-amendment approach is retained, but the deletion list is now more precisely
scoped — Article 18 is only partially deleted (paragraphs (2) to (4)), where the May text deleted "16 to 18"
wholesale. The Commission's proposed transitional derogation ("by way of derogation … continue to apply
until 31 December 2032") remains **struck out**.

**2. The following acts are repealed** [no transitional date attached to this paragraph in this version]:
- **(a)** **Chapters III, IV, V, VI, VII of Regulation (EU) 2022/868** — Data Governance Act (DGA);
- **(b)** **Regulation (EU) 2018/1807** — Free Flow of Non-Personal Data Regulation (FFNPD).

**3.** **Chapter I and II of Regulation (EU) 2022/868** (DGA) and **Directive 2019/1024** (Open Data
Directive) are repealed **[18 months after the publication in the Official Journal of the European Union]**.

▸ vs ST 9547/26: the repeals are **restructured and staggered**. The May text repealed the *whole* DGA
(2022/868), the FFNPD (2018/1807) and the Open Data Directive (2019/1024) together "with effect from [date]".
ST 10729/26 instead (i) immediately repeals only **DGA Chapters III–VII** plus the FFNPD (para 2), and
(ii) defers repeal of **DGA Chapters I–II and the Open Data Directive to 18 months after publication**
(para 3) — i.e. a phased DGA wind-down with a fixed 18-month tail for the structural chapters and the Open
Data Directive.

**4. Correlation.** References to Regulation (EU) 2022/868, Regulation (EU) 2018/1807 and Directive
2019/1024 shall be read in accordance with the **correlation table set out in Annex I** of this Regulation.
▸ Annex I (correlation table; relocations principally into the Data Act (EU) 2023/2854) is not part of this
extract.

See [repealed-instruments page](../../docs/instruments/repealed.md).

## Article 11 — Final provisions

*"This Regulation shall enter into force on the **third day** following that of its publication in the
Official Journal of the European Union."*

Staggered application (consolidated, bracketed dates as in the source):

| Provisions | Apply from |
|---|---|
| General rule (most of the Regulation) | date of entry into force |
| ePrivacy Article 5(2) rule (via Art 5(2) of this Regulation) | **6 months** after publication in the OJ |
| Article 3(8), points (a)–(c) (breach notification routing) | **18 months** after entry into force |
| Articles 6(2)–(3), 7, 8 and 9 (cyber institutional build-out) | **18 months** after entry into force |
| Obligations to report **via the national entry point** | **30 months** after entry into force |

The "via the national entry point" reporting obligations covered by the **30-month** switch-over are those
in **Article 23b of Directive (EU) 2022/2555**, Article 19a(1a), Article 24(2a) and Article 45a(3a) of
Regulation (EU) 910/2014, **Article 33(1) of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)**, Article 19(1) and (2) of
Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 (DORA), and Article 15(1) of Directive (EU) 2022/2557 (CER).

▸ vs ST 9547/26: the **30-month** national-entry-point reporting date is **unchanged** (the 24→30-month
switch-over had already happened by the May text). What changed here is that the Commission-assessment
**fallback trigger** is **deleted**: the May/earlier draft made the deferral conditional ("by way of
derogation … where the Commission finds in its assessment pursuant to Article 23a(7) … that the single-entry
point does not ensure proper functioning … "), and pointed at **Article 23(4)** NIS2. ST 10729/26 makes the
30-month date **unconditional** and re-points the cross-reference to **Article 23b** NIS2 — consistent with
the cyber-article restructuring. Until the national entry point exists, controllers notify the competent
supervisory authority directly
([GDPR Art 33 breach notification](../../docs/provisions/gdpr-art33-breach-notification.md)).

*"This Regulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States."*
