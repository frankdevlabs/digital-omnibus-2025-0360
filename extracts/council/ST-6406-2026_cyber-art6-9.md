# Extract — ST 6406/26, Articles 6–9 (cyber: NIS2, eIDAS, DORA, CER) — no operative amendment in this text

**This section is absent as operative text in the February compromise.** Unlike the May text
(ST 9547/26), ST 6406/26 contains **no enacting articles amending the NIS2 Directive (EU) 2022/2555,
the eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014, DORA (EU) 2022/2554 or the CER Directive (EU) 2022/2557**. The
document's enacting terms run from **Article 3** (GDPR) to **Article 10** (repeals); Articles 6, 7, 8
and 9 do not appear. There is therefore no ENISA "information point", no national-entry-point
architecture and no DORA/CER/eIDAS reporting-channel amendments in this version.

---

## The only cyber hook in February: a bracketed reference inside GDPR Article 33

The single point of contact with the cyber framework appears **inside the GDPR breach-notification
provision**, as a *bracketed* (i.e. not-yet-agreed) cross-reference. Consolidated GDPR Article 33(1)
(see [GDPR extract, point 8](ST-6406-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-8--article-33-breach-notification))
requires the controller to notify a high-risk breach within **96 hours**:

> *"…notify the personal data breach **[via the single-entry point established pursuant to Article 23a
> of Directive (EU) 2022/2555]** to the supervisory authority competent in accordance with Article 55
> and Article 56 of this Regulation."*

and the transitional Article 33(1a) provides that **until that single-entry point exists**, controllers
continue to notify the competent supervisory authority directly.

▸ **Feb-vs-May divergences:**
- February calls it a **"single-entry point"** under **Article 23a** of NIS2, and leaves the whole
  reference **in square brackets** (undecided in the source). By May it is the **"national entry point"**
  under **Article 23b**, and the bracketing is resolved.
- The institutional build-out that *creates* that entry point — the new NIS2 Articles 23a–23d (ENISA
  information point, national entry points, common framework, cross-border notification) and the
  parallel eIDAS/DORA/CER routing amendments — is **fully present in May** (Articles 6–9) but **entirely
  absent operatively here**. In February the entry point is only referred to, not established.

See [single-entry-point provision page](../../docs/provisions/cyber-single-entry-point.md) and the
[NIS2 instrument page](../../docs/instruments/nis2-2022-2555.md). For the May architecture, contrast
[`ST-9547-2026_cyber-art6-9.md`](ST-9547-2026_cyber-art6-9.md).
