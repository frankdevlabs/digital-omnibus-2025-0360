# Extract — ST 6406/26, ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC (no operative amendment in this text)

> **Source:** Council document **ST 6406/26** (LIMITE), Brussels, 20 February 2026 — Presidency
> compromise text, for the AGS meeting (Antici Group (Simplification)) of 27 February 2026.
> Interinstitutional file 2025/0360 (COD). **This is a working transcription, not an official text.**
> It reproduces the *consolidated* reading (Presidency tracked changes against the Commission proposal
> accepted). Always verify against the authoritative document. See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

**This section is absent as an operative article in the February text.** Unlike the May text
(ST 9547/26), the first Cyprus Presidency compromise contains **no enacting article amending Directive
2002/58/EC**. The document's enacting terms jump from **Article 3** (GDPR amendments) to **Article 10**
(repeals); there is no Article 5 (ePrivacy), and no Articles 6–9 (cyber). The subject line of the
document is "Omnibus VII – Digital (GDPR/P2B)", reflecting that this round addresses only the GDPR and
the Platform-to-Business Regulation.

---

## Where the cookie / terminal-equipment consent regime lives in this version

In February the terminal-equipment ("cookie") consent regime is placed **inside the GDPR**, as the new
**Article 88a** inserted by Article 3 point 15 — *not* in the ePrivacy Directive.
See [GDPR extract, point 15](ST-6406-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent)
and the [cookies provision page](../../docs/provisions/gdpr-art88a-88b-cookies.md).

▸ **Feb-vs-May divergence.** By May (ST 9547/26) GDPR Article 88a is **deleted** and the consent rule is
moved **back into an amended ePrivacy Article 5(3)** (with single-click refusal, a closed list of
consent-exempt purposes, a 6-month no-repeat rule and supervision by the GDPR authority). None of that
ePrivacy machinery exists operatively here — in February it is all carried by GDPR Article 88a instead.
Contrast [`ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md`](ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md).

## What the February recitals say about ePrivacy

The preamble does address Directive 2002/58/EC (consolidated sense; see
[recitals extract](ST-6406-2026_recitals.md)):

- **Recital 47** — the existing **Article 5(3) of Directive 2002/58/EC should remain applicable** insofar
  as the subscriber or user is **not a natural person**; where the subscriber/user is a natural person,
  the new GDPR Article 88a governs.
- **Recital 48** — **Article 4 of Directive 2002/58/EC should be repealed** (its security-of-processing
  and breach-notification requirements are superseded by GDPR Articles 32–34 and by NIS2). This intent is
  expressed only in the recital; there is **no operative amending article** carrying it out in this text.

▸ Because the repeal of ePrivacy Article 4 and the natural-person/non-natural-person split are stated
**only in the recitals** here (no Article 5 of the Omnibus), this file records them as `[no operative
text in ST 6406/26]`. The operative ePrivacy amendments first appear in a later compromise.
