# Extract — ST 6406/26, Article 10 (repeals and transitory clauses); Article 11 absent

> **Source:** Council document **ST 6406/26** (LIMITE), Brussels, 20 February 2026 — Presidency
> compromise text, for the AGS meeting (Antici Group (Simplification)) of 27 February 2026.
> Interinstitutional file 2025/0360 (COD). **This is a working transcription, not an official text** —
> consolidated reading. Bracketed dates are undecided in the source. Always verify against the
> authoritative document. See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

The February text's final provisions are **much thinner** than May's. Only **Article 10** (repeals)
appears operatively; there is **no Article 11 (entry into force and application)** in this document, and
**none** of the May text's other repeals (Data Governance Act, Free Flow of Non-Personal Data, Open Data
Directive) appear here. Consistent with the document's "GDPR/P2B" subject, the only repeal addressed is
the **Platform-to-Business Regulation (EU) 2019/1150**.

## Article 10 — Repeals and transitory clauses

*"1. Regulation 2019/1150/EU is repealed with effect from [date = entry into application of this
Regulation].*

*2. By way of derogation from paragraph 1, the following provisions shall continue to apply until
**31 December 2032**:*

- *(a) Article 2, point (1);*
- *(b) Article 2, point (2);*
- *(c) Article 2, point (5);*
- *(d) Article 4;*
- *(e) Article 11;*
- *(f) Article 15."*

▸ **P2B is repealed (not partially amended) in February**, but with a **derogation keeping six P2B
provisions in force until 31 December 2032** — the definitions in Article 2(1), 2(2) and 2(5), the
restriction/suspension rules in Article 4, the internal complaint-handling system in Article 11, and the
enforcement provision in Article 15. The rationale is in **recital 59** (legal certainty / protection for
business users; cross-references from other acts); see [recitals extract](ST-6406-2026_recitals.md).

▸ **Feb-vs-May divergences:**
- May (ST 9547/26) abandons the outright repeal-with-derogation and instead performs a **partial
  amendment** of P2B (deleting Articles 2(11), 2(12), 6, 8–10, 12–14, 16–18 while leaving the rest in
  force), and **drops** the 31 December 2032 transitional derogation.
- May additionally **repeals** the Data Governance Act (EU) 2022/868, the Free Flow of Non-Personal Data
  Regulation (EU) 2018/1807 and the Open Data Directive (EU) 2019/1024, and adds a **correlation table
  (Annex I)**. **None of those repeals appear in February.**
- Contrast [`ST-9547-2026_final-art10-11.md`](ST-9547-2026_final-art10-11.md) and the
  [repealed-instruments page](../../docs/instruments/repealed.md).

## Article 11 — Entry into force and application — [not present in ST 6406/26]

There is **no entry-into-force / staggered-application article** in this document; the annex ends after
Article 10 (p. 28). The application dates that the May text collects in its Article 11 (e.g. the 18-month
machine-readable-consent date, the 6-month ePrivacy date, the breach-routing and cyber build-out dates)
are, in February, expressed **only as bracketed `[OP: …]` dates inside the individual GDPR articles** —
e.g. Article 88a from **6 months**, Article 88b(1)–(2) from **24 months** and Article 88b(6) from
**48 months** after entry into force (see
[GDPR extract, point 15](ST-6406-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent)),
and the EDPB template/lists "within [nine months of entry into application]"
([point 8](ST-6406-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-8--article-33-breach-notification),
[point 9](ST-6406-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-9--article-35-dpia-lists)).
