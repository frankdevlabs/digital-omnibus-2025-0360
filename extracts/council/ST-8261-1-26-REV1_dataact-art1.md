*Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 is amended as follows:*

This Article 1 (a) amends a set of **existing** Data Act articles (points 1–17, 18a–26), and (b) inserts
three new chapters — **Chapter VIIa** (Articles 32a–32g), **Chapter VIIb** (Article 32h) and **Chapter VIIc**
(Articles 32i–32aa) — at point 18. The new chapters import the governance/registration, free-flow and
public-sector re-use regimes formerly in the Data Governance Act, the FFNPD Regulation and the Open Data
Directive into the Data Act.

<a id="point-7--article-15a"></a>
### Point 7 — New Article 15a (data availability on the basis of a public emergency)

*"Article 15a — Obligation for data holders to make data available on the basis of a public emergency"*

1. Where a public sector body, the Commission, the ECB or a Union body demonstrates an exceptional need to
   use certain data to carry out its statutory duties in the public interest when responding to, mitigating
   or supporting the recovery from a **public emergency**, it may request data holders that are legal persons
   (other than public sector bodies) to make available those data plus the metadata needed to interpret/use
   them. Such requests may also be made where the production of official statistics is required in relation
   to a public emergency.
2. Where the data are necessary to **respond** to a public emergency and the body cannot obtain them by
   other means in a timely/effective manner under equivalent conditions, the request shall concern
   **non-personal data**. Where non-personal data is insufficient to respond to the emergency, **personal
   data may also be requested** and, where possible, made available in **pseudonymised** form, subject to
   appropriate technical and organisational measures.
3. Where the data are necessary to **mitigate or support recovery** from a public emergency, a requesting
   body acting on the basis of Union or national law may request specific non-personal data. *Such requests
   shall not be made to microenterprises and small enterprises.*

▸ Re-frames B2G data sharing around **public emergencies** only; ties personal-data requests to a
necessity/insufficiency test and pseudonymisation; exempts micro/small enterprises from mitigation/recovery
requests.

## CHAPTER VIIa — data intermediation services and data altruism organisations

<a id="article-32a"></a>
### Article 32a — Public Union registers

1. The Commission shall keep and regularly update public Union registers of: (a) recognised data
   intermediation services providers and (b) recognised data altruism organisations.
2. Providers registered in the register under (a) may use the label **'data intermediation services provider
   recognised in the Union'** in written and spoken communication, as well as the common logo referred to in
   paragraph 4.
3. Organisations registered under (b) may use the label **'data altruism organisation recognised in the
   Union'**, as well as the common logo referred to in paragraph 4.
4. To ensure recognised providers/organisations are easily identifiable Union-wide, the Commission is
   empowered to adopt **implementing acts** establishing a design for the common logo, adopted under the
   **advisory procedure** referred to in Article 46(1a).

▸ Centralises DGA-style recognition into **Commission-kept Union registers** with EU labels/logo.

<a id="article-32b"></a>
### Article 32b — Competent authorities for registration

1. Each Member State shall designate one or more competent authorities for the application/enforcement of
   this Chapter in accordance with Article 37(1).
2. Those authorities shall be set up so that their **independence** from any recognised provider/organisation
   is guaranteed.

<a id="article-32c"></a>
### Article 32c — General requirements for registration of recognised data intermediation services providers

To qualify for registration in the register under Article 32a(1)(a), providers shall meet all of:
(a) not use the data for purposes other than putting them at data users' disposal; (b) collected metadata
(date, time, geolocation, duration, connections) used **only for developing that data intermediation
service**, which may entail use for **fraud detection or cybersecurity**; (c) additional tools/services
(temporary storage, curation, conversion, encryption, anonymisation/pseudonymisation **or other relevant
privacy-enhancing technologies**) used only at the explicit request/approval of the data holder/subject;
(d) where they offer services to clients **other than data intermediation services and the additional tools**
in point (c): (i) such other services explicitly requested by the data holder/subject; (ii) data not used for
other purposes; (iii) data intermediation through **an entity functionally separate from entities offering
other services**; (iv) not designated a gatekeeper under Art 3 of Reg. (EU) 2022/1925; (v) commercial terms
not dependent on use of **other services** by the provider or a related entity; (e) act in data subjects'
best interest; (f) ensure **fair, transparent, non-discriminatory access** to the service; (g) maintain a
**log record** of the intermediation activity; (h) take security measures for storage/processing/transmission
of non-personal data. **Points (d)(iii) and (iv) do not apply to micro and small sized enterprises.**

▸ DGA Article 12 requirements re-cast; "value-added services" terminology generalised to "other services";
new fairness-of-access (f), log-record (g) and security (h) duties added.

<a id="article-32d"></a>
### Article 32d — General requirements for registration of recognised data altruism organisations

To qualify under Article 32a(1)(b), an organisation shall: (a) carry out data altruism activities; (b) be a
legal person established under national law to meet objectives of general interest; (c) operate on a
**not-for-profit** basis and be legally independent from any for-profit entity; (d) carry out its data
altruism activities through a structure **functionally separate** from its other activities.

<a id="article-32e"></a>
### Article 32e — Registration in public Union register

1. A provider/organisation meeting Article 32c / 32d may apply to the competent authority of the Member State
   of its **main establishment**.
2. Providers/organisations with **no main establishment in the Union** shall designate a **legal
   representative** in a Member State (deemed under that State's jurisdiction).
3. Competent authorities shall establish the necessary application forms.
4. Where all information is submitted and the provider complies with Article 32c, the competent authority
   shall decide within **12 weeks** of receipt. The time limit is **suspended** pending requested additional
   information, and may be **extended once by a maximum of 12 weeks** in duly justified cases (complexity or
   novelty), with reasons. On compliance, the authority submits the information to the Commission, which
   registers the provider. The same applies to data altruism organisations (Article 32d). Registration is
   **valid in all Member States**.
5. The competent authority may charge **proportionate, objective** registration fees based on monitoring
   costs; for small mid-caps, SMEs and start-ups it may **discount or waive** the fee.
6. Registered entities shall notify changes / cessation of activities.
7. The competent authority shall notify the Commission electronically without delay; the Commission updates
   the register without undue delay.

▸ Deadlines image-verified: **12 weeks** to decide, extendable **once by a maximum of 12 weeks**.

<a id="article-32f"></a>
### Article 32f — Duties of recognised data altruism organisations

1. Inform data subjects/holders before processing of (a) the objectives of general interest and any specified
   purpose for which personal data is processed, and (b) the location of processing and the general-interest
   objectives for any third-country processing.
2. Not use data for objectives other than those for which the data subject/holder allowed processing; no
   misleading marketing to solicit data.
3. Provide **electronic means** for obtaining and withdrawing consent/permissions.
4. Inform data holders without delay of any unauthorised transfer/access/use of the non-personal data shared.
5. Where they facilitate third-party processing, specify, where relevant, the third country of intended use.

<a id="article-32g"></a>
### Article 32g — Monitoring of compliance

1. Competent authorities monitor and supervise whether recognised providers/organisations comply with this
   Chapter (incl. continued registration compliance); may also act on a request by a natural/legal person.
2. Power to request all information necessary to verify compliance (proportionate and reasoned).
3. On finding non-compliance, notify the entity and give it the opportunity to state its views within
   **30 days** of receipt of the notification.
4. Power to require cessation of non-compliance immediately or within a reasonable time limit, taking
   appropriate/proportionate measures.
5. If non-compliance persists after notification, the entity shall (a) lose the right to use the label and
   (b) be removed from the public Union register. Any decision revoking the label shall be made public and
   notified to the Commission, which removes the entity from the register.

## CHAPTER VIIb — Free flow of non-personal data within the Union

<a id="article-32h"></a>
### Article 32h — Prohibition of localisation requirements for non-personal data within the Union

1. **Data localisation requirements for non-personal data shall be prohibited**, unless justified on grounds
   of **public security** in compliance with proportionality, or laid down on the basis of Union law.
2. Member States shall **immediately communicate** to the Commission any **draft act** introducing or
   changing a data localisation requirement, in accordance with Articles 5, 6 and 7 of Directive (EU)
   2015/1535.

▸ Re-enacts the FFNPD Regulation free-flow rule. Chapter title: "Free flow of non-personal data **within**
the Union" ("within" replaces the Commission's "in"); image-confirmed (p62).

<a id="chapter-viic"></a>
## CHAPTER VIIc — Re-use of data and documents held by public sector bodies

### Section 1 — General Provisions

<a id="article-32i"></a>
### Article 32i — Subject matter and scope

1. This Chapter governs the re-use and the practical arrangements for facilitating re-use of: **(a)** existing
   data/documents held by **public sector bodies** of the Member States, including certain categories of
   protected data; **(b)** existing data/documents held by **public undertakings** that are (i) active in the
   areas of Chapter II of Directive 2014/25/EU; (ii) public service operators under Art 2 of Reg. (EC) No
   1370/2007; (iii) air carriers with PSOs under Art 16 of Reg. (EC) No 1008/2008; or (iv) Community
   shipowners with PSOs under Art 4 of Reg. (EEC) No 3577/92; **(c)** research data per the conditions in
   Article 32t.
2. Exclusions from the Chapter: (a) data outside the public task; (b) public-undertaking data produced
   outside services of general interest / related to activities directly exposed to competition (Art 34 of
   Dir. 2014/25/EU); (c) data excluded on grounds of national security, defence or public security; (d) data
   held by public service broadcasters and their subsidiaries.
3. Exclusions from **Section 2**: (a) data excluded on grounds of statistical/commercial confidentiality;
   (b) access-restricted data, incl. on grounds of personal-data protection; (c) logos, crests and insignia;
   (d) third-party IP-protected data/documents; (e) data held by cultural establishments other than
   libraries (incl. university libraries), museums and archives; (f) data held by educational establishments
   of secondary level and below, and (for other educational establishments) data other than research data
   under paragraph 1(c); (g) data other than research data held by research-performing/funding organisations;
   (h) data excluded on grounds of critical-entity/critical-infrastructure protection (Dir. (EU) 2022/2557).
4. Exclusions from **Section 3**: (a) data that are not certain categories of protected data; (b) public-
   undertaking data; (c) cultural-/educational-establishment data; (d) data covered by Section 2. *Section 3
   creates no obligation on public sector bodies to allow re-use, nor releases them from confidentiality
   obligations.*
5–9. Builds on and is without prejudice to Union/national access regimes; subject to international IP
   agreements (Berne, TRIPS, WCT); the database-maker's right (Art 7(1) of Dir. 96/9/EC) shall not be used to
   prevent re-use; governs existing data incl. that under Dir. 2007/2/EC; without prejudice to protection of
   the categories in Article 2(54).

▸ Re-enacts the Open Data Directive scope into the Data Act.

<a id="article-32j"></a>
### Article 32j — Non-discrimination

Conditions for re-use shall be non-discriminatory, transparent, proportionate and objectively justified and
shall not restrict competition (applies equally to cross-border re-use). Where a public sector body re-uses
data as input for commercial activities outside its public tasks, the same charges/conditions apply as to
other re-users.

<a id="article-32k"></a>
### Article 32k — Exclusive arrangements

1. Re-use shall be open to all potential market actors; agreements granting **exclusive rights** or
   restricting availability for re-use are **prohibited**.
2. Derogation where an exclusive right is necessary for a service of general interest — subject to:
   transparency/equal-treatment conditions; publication online; (c) review of the justification **every three
   years** (except cultural-digitisation exclusivity); (d) arrangements made public online at least **two
   months** before taking effect.
3. For digitisation of cultural resources, the exclusivity period shall **in general not exceed 10 years**;

<a id="article-32l"></a>
### Article 32l — General principles relating to charging

1. Any charges shall be transparent, non-discriminatory, proportionate, objectively justified and shall not
   restrict competition.
2. For standard charges, conditions and actual amounts (incl. calculation basis) established in advance and

<a id="article-32la"></a>
### Article 32la — Procedure for requests for re-use *(new)*

1. Public sector bodies shall process requests by electronic means where possible, and make the data/document
   available (or finalise the licence offer) within a reasonable time consistent with access-request
   time-frames.
2. Unless shorter national time limits apply, public sector bodies (and, for protected data, the competent
   bodies under Article 32z(1)) shall deliver the data/documents (or finalise the licence offer) as soon as

### Subsection 3 — Conditions for re-use

<a id="article-32p"></a>
### Article 32p — Available formats

1. Without prejudice to Subsection 5, public sector bodies and public undertakings shall make data/documents
   available by electronic means in formats that are **open, machine-readable, accessible, findable and
   re-usable**, together with their metadata; format and metadata shall, where possible, comply with **formal
   open standards**; documents available in any pre-existing format/language.
2. Where possible, produce/make available such data per the principle of **'open by design and by default'**.
3. No obligation to create/adapt data or provide extracts where this would involve disproportionate effort.
4. No obligation to continue producing/storing a certain type of data for the purpose of re-use.
5. Dynamic data made available **immediately after collection** via suitable APIs and, where relevant, as a
   bulk download.
6. Where immediate availability would exceed financial/technical capacities (disproportionate effort),
   dynamic data made available within a time frame / with temporary technical restrictions not unduly
   impairing exploitation.
7. Paragraphs 1–6 apply to existing public-undertaking data available for re-use.
8. **High-value datasets** (listed under Article 32v(1)) made available in machine-readable format, via
   suitable APIs and, where relevant, as a bulk download.

<a id="article-32q"></a>
### Article 32q — Principles governing charging for public sector data and documents

1. Re-use shall be **free of charge**; recovery of **marginal costs** of reproduction/provision/dissemination
   (and anonymisation / protection of confidential information) may be allowed.
2. Paragraph 1 does not apply to: (a) public sector bodies required to generate revenue to cover a
   substantial part of their costs; (b) libraries (incl. university libraries), museums and archives;
   (c) public undertakings.
3. Member States shall publish online a list of the bodies under paragraph 2(a).
4. For 2(a) and 2(c) bodies, total charges calculated on objective/transparent/verifiable criteria; total
   income not exceeding cost of collection/production/reproduction/dissemination/data storage plus a
   **reasonable return on investment**.
5. For 2(b) bodies, total income not exceeding cost (incl. preservation and rights clearance) plus a
   reasonable return on investment.
6. Charges higher than paragraphs 1, 4 and 5 may be set for re-use by **very large enterprises** —
   proportionate, on objective criteria taking account of economic power / ability to acquire data (incl. a
   gatekeeper designation under Reg. (EU) 2022/1925).
7. Free of charge for the re-user: (a) high-value datasets (subject to Article 32v(3),(4),(5)); (b) research
   data under Article 32i(1)(c).

<a id="article-32r"></a>
### Article 32r — Standard licences

1. Re-use not subject to conditions unless objective, proportionate, non-discriminatory and justified on a
   public-interest objective.
2. Conditions shall not unnecessarily restrict re-use or restrict competition.
3. In Member States using licences, standard licences (adaptable) shall be available in digital, electronically
   processable format.
4. Public sector bodies and public undertakings may establish **special conditions for very large
   enterprises** — proportionate, on objective criteria (incl. gatekeeper designation under Reg. (EU)
   2022/1925).

<a id="article-32s"></a>
### Article 32s — Practical arrangements

1. Member States shall make practical arrangements facilitating the search for re-usable data (asset lists
   with metadata, online/machine-readable, on data portals linked to the asset lists); facilitate
   cross-linguistic search (metadata aggregation at Union level); encourage preservation arrangements.
2. Member States shall ensure simple access to datasets (single point of access; progressively making
   available suitable datasets, incl. data held by Union institutions, in accessible/findable/re-usable
   electronic formats).

### Subsection 4 — Research data

<a id="article-32t"></a>
### Article 32t — Research data

1. Member States shall support availability of research data via **open access policies** (publicly funded
   research data openly available), following 'open by default' and FAIR principles, taking account of IP,
   personal-data protection, confidentiality, security and legitimate commercial interests ('as open as
   possible, as closed as necessary'); addressed to research-performing and research-funding organisations.
2. Subject to Article 32i(3)(d), research data shall be **re-usable for commercial or non-commercial
   purposes** insofar as publicly funded and already made publicly available through an institutional or
   subject-based repository (legitimate commercial interests, knowledge-transfer and pre-existing IP taken
   into account).

### Subsection 5 — High-value datasets

<a id="article-32u"></a>
### Article 32u — Thematic categories of high-value datasets

1. The thematic categories shall be as set out in **Annex I**.
2. The Commission is empowered to adopt **delegated acts** under Article 45(2) to amend Annex I by adding new
   thematic categories reflecting technological/market developments.

<a id="article-32v"></a>

   archives.

<a id="article-32w"></a>
### Section 3 — Re-use of certain categories of protected data held by public sector bodies

### Article 32w — Conditions for re-use of protected data

1. Public sector bodies competent to grant/refuse access for re-use of certain categories of protected data
   shall make public the conditions and procedure via the **single information point** (Article 32aa); may be
   assisted by the competent bodies (Article 32z(1)). Member States shall ensure bodies are adequately
   resourced.
2. Re-use shall not affect the protected nature and shall be allowed only: (a) in compliance with IP rights;
   (b) where confidential data is not disclosed (unless re-use is allowed on the data subject's consent or
   data holder's permission per paragraph 5); (c) in compliance with Reg. (EU) 2016/679.
3. To preserve the protected nature, public sector bodies may require: (a) prior anonymisation (personal
   data) / modification, aggregation or other disclosure control (commercially confidential or IP-protected
   information); (b) remote access within a **secure processing environment**; (c) on-site access where
   remote access cannot be allowed without jeopardising third-party rights.
4. The public sector body reserves the right to verify processing and to prohibit use of results that
   jeopardise third-party rights (decision comprehensible/transparent); re-use conditional on a
   **confidentiality obligation**; on unauthorised re-use of non-personal data, the re-user must inform
   affected persons without delay.
5. Where re-use cannot otherwise be allowed, it may proceed (a) with the data subject's consent or (b) with
   the data holder's permission; the public sector body shall make best efforts to assist re-users in
   obtaining such consent/permission where feasible without disproportionate burden (may be assisted by the
   competent bodies under Article 32z).

<a id="article-32x"></a>
### Article 32x — Requirements for transfers of non-personal data to third countries by re-users

1. A re-user intending to transfer non-personal certain-categories-of-protected data to a **third country**

   affected persons of the intention, purpose and safeguards; re-use not allowed unless the affected person
   gives permission for the transfer.
2. Public sector bodies shall transmit non-personal confidential / IP-protected data to a re-user intending a
   third-country transfer only if the re-user contractually commits to (a) complying with IP / commercial /
   statistical-confidentiality obligations even after transfer; (b) accepting the jurisdiction of the courts
   of the transmitting Member State.
3. The Commission may adopt **implementing acts** establishing model contractual clauses (examination
   procedure, Article 46(2)).
4. Public sector bodies shall, where relevant, provide guidance/assistance to re-users.
5. Where justified by a substantial number of requests, the Commission may adopt **implementing acts**
   declaring that a third country's legal/supervisory/enforcement arrangements (a) ensure essentially
   equivalent IP / trade-secret protection; (b) are effectively applied/enforced; and (c) provide effective
   judicial redress (examination procedure, paragraph 6).
6. *(examination-procedure paragraph for the adequacy acts.)*
7. Specific Union legislative acts may deem certain non-personal data categories **highly sensitive**; the
   Commission shall then adopt **delegated acts** (Article 45) laying down special transfer conditions. A
