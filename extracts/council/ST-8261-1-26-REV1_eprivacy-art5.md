# Extract — ST 8261/1/26 REV 1, Article 5 (amendments to the ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC)

> **Source:** Council document **ST 8261/1/26 REV 1** (LIMITE), Brussels, 15 April 2026 — Presidency revised
> compromise text, for the AGS meeting of 24 April 2026. Interinstitutional file 2025/0360 (COD).
> **This is a working transcription, not an official text** — consolidated reading (tracked changes against
> the Commission proposal accepted). Salient changes are flagged with **▸**. Bracketed `[…]` dates are
> undecided in the source. Always verify against the authoritative document. See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

**Why this file matters — and what is new here.** This is the **first** compromise version to carry an
**operative** ePrivacy article. February (ST 6406/26) had **no** Article 5 of the Omnibus at all — the
cookie / terminal-equipment consent regime sat inside the GDPR as the new **Article 88a**
([see Feb GDPR extract, point 15](ST-6406-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent)).
This April text **deletes GDPR Article 88a**
([see this version's GDPR extract](ST-8261-1-26-REV1_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent))
and **moves the consent regime back into an amended ePrivacy Directive** (Article 5(3)), as later carried
into May (ST 9547/26). See [cookies provision](../../docs/provisions/gdpr-art88a-88b-cookies.md) and the
[ePrivacy instrument page](../../docs/instruments/eprivacy-2002-58.md).

*Directive 2002/58/EC is amended as follows:*

---

## Point 1 — Article 4 deleted

Article 4 (security of services; breach notification by providers of electronic communications services) is
**deleted** — the subject matter is covered by NIS2 (Directive (EU) 2022/2555) and by GDPR Articles 32–34
(see recital 48, [recitals extract](ST-8261-1-26-REV1_recitals.md)).

---

<a id="article-5-3-terminal-equipment-consent"></a>
## Point 2 — Article 5(3) replaced (terminal-equipment consent)

The chapeau, consolidated, reads *"In Article 5, paragraph 3 is replaced by the following:"*

*"3. Member States shall ensure that the storing of information, or gaining of access to information already
stored, in the terminal equipment of a natural person is only allowed when that person has given his or her
**consent**, in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679.*

*Storing of information, or gaining of access to information already stored, in the terminal equipment of a
natural person without consent, and subsequent processing for the same purpose, shall be lawful to the
extent it is **necessary** for any of the following purposes:*

- *a) carrying out the transmission of an electronic communication over an electronic communications
  network, providing a service explicitly requested by the user;*
- *b) creating **anonymous aggregated** information about the usage of an online service requested by the
  user to **measure the audience** of such a service, where it is carried out by the provider of that online
  service, or by a third party acting on behalf of this provider, solely for the provider's own use;*
- *c) maintaining or restoring the **technical security** of the interface necessary for the provision of a
  service requested by the user or the technical security of the terminal equipment used for the provision
  of such service;*
- *d) **measuring the display and performance of advertising** made solely on the basis of the immediate
  content displayed on the user's interface and **not based on any type of profiling**.*

*The user shall be able to **refuse** requests for consent in an easy and intelligible manner with a
**single-click button or equivalent means**. If the user gives consent, the provider shall not make a new
request for consent for the same purpose for the period during which the controller can lawfully rely on the
consent of the data subject. If the data subject **refuses** a request for consent, the controller shall not
make a new request for consent for the same purpose for a period of **at least six months**."*

▸ **Key April-specific features and Feb → Apr → May movement:**
- **Newly operative here.** The whole ePrivacy Art 5(3) regime appears for the first time in this version
  (February had it only inside GDPR Art 88a).
- **Four** consent-exempt purposes, including a **(d) contextual-advertising-measurement** exemption ("not
  based on any type of profiling"). **May drops this advertising exemption** and instead has (a) transmission;
  (b) explicitly-requested service; (c) anonymous first-party audience measurement; (d) security incl.
  cybersecurity, data-protection, privacy and **fraud prevention**
  ([contrast May ePrivacy](ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md)). April combines transmission + requested-service
  into a single point (a), where May splits them.
- April uses "to the extent it is **necessary**"; **May tightens this to "strictly necessary"**.
- April's Art 5(3) does **not** include the express clause (added by May) designating the **GDPR supervisory
  authority** for supervision/enforcement of this paragraph.
- Single-click refusal and the **6-month** no-repeat rule are present here, as in May.

---

## Point 2a — Article 17 new paragraph (transposition)

Added: *"3. Member States shall adopt and publish, by **[18 months after the adoption of this Regulation]**
the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary to comply with Article 5(3). They shall
immediately communicate the text of those measures to the Commission. They should apply those measures from
[18 months after the adoption of this Regulation]."*
▸ April sets the ePrivacy transposition deadline at **18 months**; **May moves it to 24 months**
([May ePrivacy, point 2a](ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md)). Note also a tension within this version: the
final-provisions article makes **Article 5(2) of the Omnibus** (i.e. this Art 5(3) replacement) apply
**6 months** after publication ([final provisions](ST-8261-1-26-REV1_final-art10-11.md#article-11-final-provisions)),
while this transposition clause states 18 months — the dates are still in flux.
