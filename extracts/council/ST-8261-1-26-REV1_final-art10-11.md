# Extract — ST 8261/1/26 REV 1, Articles 10–11 (amendments, repeals, transitional & final provisions)

> **Source:** Council document **ST 8261/1/26 REV 1** (LIMITE), Brussels, 17 April 2026 — Presidency revised
> compromise text, for the AGS meeting of 24 April 2026. Interinstitutional file 2025/0360 (COD).
> **This is a working transcription, not an official text** — consolidated reading. Bracketed `[…]` dates
> are undecided in the source. Always verify against the authoritative document. See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

**What is new here.** Unlike February (ST 6406/26), which had only a thin Article 10 (P2B repeal) and **no
Article 11** ([see Feb final extract](ST-6406-2026_final-art10-11.md)), this April text carries the full
final provisions: a P2B **partial amendment**, the further repeals (DGA, FFNPD, Open Data Directive) with a
correlation table, and a staggered **entry-into-force / application** article.

---

<a id="article-10-amendments-repeals-transitory"></a>
## Article 10 — Amendments, repeals and transitory clauses

**1. Platform-to-Business Regulation (EU) 2019/1150 — amended (not repealed):**
- **(a)** Articles **2(11), 2(12), 6, 8 to 10, 12 to 14, and 16 to 18** are **deleted as of [date of entry
  into force]**.
- **(b)** Articles **4 and 11** are **deleted as of 1 January 2033**.
▸ **Feb → Apr → May movement on P2B:**
- **February:** P2B **repealed** outright, with a derogation keeping six provisions (Art 2(1), 2(2), 2(5), 4,
  11, 15) until **31 December 2032**.
- **April (this text):** abandons the outright repeal for a **partial amendment** — deleting the same block
  of articles that May deletes (2(11), 2(12), 6, 8–10, 12–14, 16–18) immediately, **but additionally** sets
  a delayed deletion of **Articles 4 and 11 on 1 January 2033** (echoing February's transitional intent for
  the business-user protections, now expressed as a sunset date). The Commission's "continue to apply until
  31 December 2032" derogation paragraph is **struck**. (Note: recital 59 of this same text still narrates
  the older "remain in application until 31 December 2032" formulation for Articles 4 and 11 — the
  recital/operative dates are not fully reconciled; see [recitals](ST-8261-1-26-REV1_recitals.md#recital-59-p2b).)
- **May:** keeps the partial amendment but **drops the 1 January 2033 deletion of Articles 4 and 11**
  ([contrast May final, Article 10](ST-9547-2026_final-art10-11.md#article-10-amendments-repeals-transitory)).

*(The February para "2" derogation listing the six surviving P2B provisions is struck in this text;
consolidated Article 10 runs 1, 3, 4.)*

**3. The following acts are repealed**, with effect from [Date, aligned with the entry into application of
the amendments]:
- **(a) Regulation (EU) 2022/868** — Data Governance Act (DGA);
- **(b) Regulation (EU) 2018/1807** — Free Flow of Non-Personal Data Regulation (FFNPD);
- **(c) Directive 2019/1024** — Open Data Directive.
▸ **New vs February** (none of these repeals appear in the February text).

**4. Correlation.** References to Regulation (EU) 2022/868, Regulation (EU) 2018/1807 and Directive
2019/1024 shall be read in accordance with the **correlation table set out in Annex I** of this Regulation.
▸ Annex I (correlation table) is not part of this extract.

See [repealed-instruments page](../../docs/instruments/repealed.md).

<a id="article-11-final-provisions"></a>
## Article 11 — Final provisions (entry into force and application)

*"This Regulation shall enter into force on the **third day** following that of its publication in the
Official Journal of the European Union."*

Staggered application (consolidated; bracketed/undecided figures as in the source):

| Provisions | Apply from |
|---|---|
| General rule (most of the Regulation) | date of entry into force |
| **Articles under Chapter VIIc** (Data Act chapter inserted by Article 1) | **18 months** after publication |
| **Article 5(2)** of this Regulation (the ePrivacy Art 5(3) consent rule) | **6 months** after publication |
| **Article 3(8), points (a) to (c)** (GDPR breach-notification routing) + **Articles 6(2) and (3), 7, 8 and 9** (cyber build-out) | **18 months** from entry into force |
| Reporting **via the national entry point** under NIS2 Art 23(4), eIDAS Art 19a(1a)/24(2a)/45a(3a), GDPR Art 33(1), DORA Art 19(1)–(2), CER Art 15(1) | **24 months** from entry into force |

The reporting-switchover row carries a conditional trigger, consolidated: *"By way of derogation from the
first sentence, where the Commission finds in its **Decision** pursuant to Article 23a(7) of Directive (EU)
2022/2555 that the single-entry point does not ensure the proper functioning, reliability, integrity or
confidentiality, **or where the Commission adopts no such Decision**, the obligations to report via the
single-entry point … shall enter into application 24 months from the entry into force of this Regulation."*

▸ **Feb → Apr → May movement:**
- The actual reporting switch-over is **24 months** in April; **May moves it to 30 months**
  ([May final, Article 11](ST-9547-2026_final-art10-11.md#article-11--entry-into-force-and-application)).
- **Drafting not reconciled:** this Article 11 still speaks of the **"single-entry point"** and of **"Article
  23a(7)"** of NIS2, whereas the operative cyber articles of this same text use the **"single-information
  point" / "national entry point"** model and the consolidated Article 23a has no paragraph (7) — see
  [cyber extract](ST-8261-1-26-REV1_cyber-art6-9.md). Until the national entry point exists, controllers
  notify the competent supervisory authority directly
  ([Art 33(1a)](ST-8261-1-26-REV1_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-8--article-33-breach-notification)).

*"This Regulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States."*
